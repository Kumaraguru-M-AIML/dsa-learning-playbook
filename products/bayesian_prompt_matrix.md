## 🌀 FRAMEWORK 1: The Bayesian Update Chain (Self-Correction)
**Use Case:** Complex logic, code debugging, strategic planning.
**Mechanism:** Forces the AI to treat its initial answer as a "Prior Belief" and run mathematical counter-evidence checks to update its final response.

### 📋 The Prompt (Copy & Paste Below)
```text
[ROLE: BAYESIAN CRITIC AGENT]
Initialize a Multi-Cycle Bayesian Correction loop for the following objective: 
"{INSERT_YOUR_GOAL_OR_PROBLEM_HERE}"

You must strictly execute these four cognitive phases:

Phase 1: THE INITIAL PRIOR
- Generate your primary, direct answer to the objective. Do not overthink yet.

Phase 2: THE COUNTER-EVIDENCE HARVEST
- Actively seek 3 systemic flaws, unstated assumptions, or logical failures in your Phase 1 answer. 
- Score the probability of each flaw being true (P(E)).

Phase 3: THE BAYESIAN UPDATE
- Treat Phase 1 as P(A) (Prior Probability). 
- Using the counter-evidence from Phase 2, mathematically or logically recalculate your confidence in the initial answer. 
- State the new posterior confidence percentage (e.g., "Updated from 90% to 74% due to X assumption").

Phase 4: THE REFINED SYNTHESIS
- Output the final, hardened answer, factoring in the mitigation of all identified flaws.
```
