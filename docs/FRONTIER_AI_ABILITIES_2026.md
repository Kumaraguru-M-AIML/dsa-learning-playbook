## 🎯 1. Core Architectural Abilities

### 🤖 1.1 Agentic Autonomy & Workflow Orchestration
The most significant leap in the current generation is "agency"—the ability for models to break down goals, utilize external tools, and operate independently over long periods.

* **Claude Opus 4.6/4.7**: Acts as a complete project manager and execution team. It can assemble parallel "agent teams" (e.g., an Architect, multiple Implementers, an Auditor, and a Documenter) to work on different modules of a project simultaneously.
* **Gemini 3.1 Pro/Ultra**: Features dedicated endpoints specifically for agentic workflows. It is highly skilled at using terminal, bash, and custom tools to build complex multi-step pipelines, such as configuring live APIs into real-time data dashboards.
* **GLM-5/5.1 & Kimi K2.6**: These models offer highly capable multimodal agent swarms capable of executing workflows over long contexts (256K+ tokens), making them cost-effective options for autonomous tasks.

### 💻 1.2 Advanced Software Engineering & "Vibe Coding"
If you are leveraging vibe coding to rapidly prototype ambitious applications—perhaps orchestrating something as complex as a custom sovereign protocol or an apex-tier system—these models have shifted from basic autocomplete to full codebase management.

* **GPT-5.4/5.5**: Absorbed the capabilities of Codex directly into its flagship model. With its 1M+ token context window, it can digest entire multi-file architectures, generate full-stack scaffolding, and debug complex, gnarly issues across thousands of lines of code.
* **DeepSeek V4 Pro**: A massive open-weight contender scoring **80.6% on SWE-bench**, proving it can autonomously resolve real-world software engineering issues at a frontier level.
* **Claude Sonnet 4.6 & Gemma 4**: Optimized for speed and rapid, iterative development, balancing high-throughput inference with strong coding logic (with Gemma hitting a coding Elo of 2,150).

### 🧠 1.3 Deep Reasoning & Systems Thinking
Models are now evaluated heavily on their ability to deduce and solve novel problems rather than regurgitating memorized data. For applying systems thinking to architect complex mental models, these models are top-tier.

* **Gemini 3.1 Pro**: Scored an exceptional **77.1% on the ARC-AGI-2** benchmark, showing a profound ability to process entirely new logic patterns and bridge the gap between complex raw data and user-friendly synthesis.
* **Claude Mythos**: A restricted, highly specialized model focused purely on advanced, deep-chain logical reasoning and expert-level analysis.
* **Meta Muse Spark**: Introduces a unique "Contemplating mode" that launches multiple internal agents to debate, refine, and aggregate solutions. It heavily dominates in visual reasoning (**86.4% on CharXiv**), such as interpreting complex scientific charts, figures, and health data.

### 🧮 1.4 Mathematical Mastery
* **GPT-5.4 Flagship**: Dominates structured mathematics with **94.6% on MATH-500**, excelling in algebraic reasoning, number theory, and calculus. Interestingly, its smaller, highly efficient variants often excel at competition-style logic.
* **GLM-5**: Extremely proficient in advanced mathematics, scoring **92.7% on the AIME** benchmark, proving that open-access models are successfully closing the gap in specialized, rigorous logic.

### 📚 1.5 Massive Context Synthesizers
The ability to hold information in "working memory" has expanded exponentially through efficient Mixture of Experts (MoE) architectures. This is incredibly useful if you are building deep, gamified frameworks (like RPG-style habit or progression trackers) that require the AI to remember vast amounts of historical data without losing the thread.

* **Llama 4 Scout**: Features an unprecedented **10 Million token** context window. It can read, analyze, and synthesize entire libraries of documents, massive datasets, or endless codebase logs in a single prompt.
* **Claude Opus 4.6**: Features "context compaction," automatically summarizing older conversational context as you approach the 1M token limit. This allows it to maintain long-running, persistent tasks without "forgetting" instructions.

* **Grok 4/4.20**: Built for extreme speed and real-time conversational intelligence (**Arena Elo 1,495**), making it highly effective for live data processing and streaming responses.
* **NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Super**: Prioritizes raw processing power with **2.2x throughput** while maintaining a solid **60.5% SWE-bench** score, making it a powerhouse for high-volume, automated technical tasks.
