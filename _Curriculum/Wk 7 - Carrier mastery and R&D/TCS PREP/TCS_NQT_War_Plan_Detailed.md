# TCS NQT: Detailed Exam Intelligence & Strategy Report

This report is a deep-dive analysis of the **TCS NQT (National Qualifier Test)** based on the indexed resources in your project. It maps the structure, the elimination logic, and the high-yield topics required to secure a **Digital/Prime** role.

---

## 1. The Survival Strategy: "Speed + Segmentation"

The exam is designed to **eliminate, not select**. To survive, your strategy must be:
1.  **Surgical Precision:** Spend zero time on low-probability topics (e.g., complex geometry) and 100% effort on high-frequency clusters (Arithmetic).
2.  **The "Two-Sweep" Rule:** 
    *   **Sweep 1:** Solve every easy MCQ/FUB in under 45 seconds.
    *   **Sweep 2:** Return to "Moderate" puzzles only once the foundation is secured.
3.  **Digital/Prime Focus:** The Foundation covers the "Ninja" role, but the **Advanced Section** is the gatekeeper for the higher salary packages. You must perform in both.

---

## 2. R1 Structure: The Pipeline

Round 1 is the **NQT Test**. It is divided into two major tracks: **Foundation** and **Advanced**.

### A. FOUNDATION SECTION
*   **Purpose:** Primary screening (Eliminates ~60-70% of candidates).
*   **Format:** Mostly MCQs.

| Section | Key Categories | Specific High-Yield Topics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Numerical Ability** | **Arithmetic (70%)**, Number Theory, Statistics | Percentages, Profit & Loss, Ratio & Proportion, Time & Work, Speed-Time-Distance, Averages, Divisibility, HCF/LCM, Data Interpretation (Pie/Table) |
| **Reasoning Ability** | **Logic & Puzzles**, Spatial, Analytical | Coding-Decoding, Number Series, Seating Arrangement (Circular/Linear), Blood Relations, Direction Sense, Syllogisms, Venn Diagrams |
| **Verbal Ability** | **Grammar & Context**, Comprehension | Error Identification, Sentence Completion (Fillers), Paragraph/Sentence Ordering, Synonyms/Antonyms, Reading Comprehension |

### B. ADVANCED SECTION
*   **Purpose:** Categorization (Determines if you get Digital/Prime or Ninja).
*   **Format:** MCQs + **FUB (Fill-Up-Box)** where no options are provided.

| Section | Key Categories | Specific High-Yield Topics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Advanced Quant** | **Higher Math**, Probability | Permutations & Combinations (P&C), Probability, Logarithms, Compound Interest, Progressions (AP/GP/HP), Partnership, Mensuration |
| **Advanced Reasoning** | **Complex Logic**, Critical Thinking | Multi-condition Puzzles, Data Sufficiency (Numerical/Logical), Advanced Seating, Statement-Assumption/Conclusion |
| **Coding** | **Programming (2 Problems)** | **Array Manipulation, String Processing**, Pattern Logic, Math-based Algorithms (Prime, Fibonacci, etc.) |

## 3. Top Tier Topic Clusters (90% Probability)

Derived from `TCS NQT (Numerical Ability) Official Memory Based Paper 2020` and `TCS-NQT_NINJA_2019-2020.pdf`.

### 🧮 Numerical Cluster (The "Marks Maker")
*   **Profit & Loss (Weight Loss/Grapes to Raisins):** A favorite TCS pattern.
*   **Time & Work (Pipes & Cisterns / Alternative Days):** Essential for speed marks.
*   **Divisibility Logic:** Solving for `7m` or `12.95m` patterns.
*   **Statistics:** Finding the Mean Deviation or Standard Deviation (Often asked in Advanced).

### 🧩 Reasoning Cluster (The "Time Sink")
*   **Seating Arrangement:** 6-8 people in a circle or facing North/South. (Master the "Family Tree + Seating" hybrid).
*   **Syllogism:** Master the Venn Diagram method for "Some/No/All" statements.
*   **Coding Deductive Logic:** Letter shifts (e.g., APPLE -> BQQMF).

### 💻 Coding Cluster
*   **Languages:** C, C++, Java, Python, Perl.
*   **Focus:** **Strings and Arrays**. 90% of TCS NQT coding problems rotate around sorting, reversing, filtering, or counting patterns within strings and arrays.

---

## 4. Elimination Factors (Hidden Traps)

1.  **Fill-in-the-Box (FUB):** In Advanced sections, you cannot guess. One calculation error = 0 marks.
2.  **Sectional Switch:** You can switch between sections *within* the Foundation round, but not between Foundation and Advanced.
3.  **Time Management:** Spending >2 minutes on a single puzzle is an automatic loss.
