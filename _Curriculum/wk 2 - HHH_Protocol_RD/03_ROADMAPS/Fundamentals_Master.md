# PART 1: MOVEMENT FUNDAMENTALS

## 1.1 THE TENSION PRINCIPLE (Pavel, Wade)

### The Core Law:
**Strength = Tension Generation Skill**

- Strength is NOT about muscle size. It's about your nervous system's ability to generate HIGH TENSION.
- **Pavel's Law:** "Strength is built by tensing muscles HARDER, not by exhausting them with reps."
- **Wade's Law:** "High resistance + Mental focus on contraction = Strength."

### The Fundamental Practice:
1.  **Low Reps (1-5):** High tension cannot be maintained beyond 5 reps.
2.  **Never to Failure:** Failure trains failure. Stop with 1-2 reps in reserve.
3.  **Frequent Practice:** Strength is a SKILL. Practice daily, not weekly.

---

## 1.2 THE PROGRESSIVE RESISTANCE PRINCIPLE (Wade, Sommer)

### The Core Law:
**To get stronger, resistance must increase over time.**

- **With Bodyweight:** Change leverage, not reps.
  - Example: Wall Pushup → Floor Pushup → One-Arm Pushup (NOT 100 pushups).
- **The 10-Step Rule (Wade):** Every exercise has 10 progressive steps from beginner to master.

### The Fundamental Practice:
1.  **Master Step 1 First:** Do NOT skip steps. The foundation is everything.
2.  **Progression Standard:** Only advance when you can do 3 sets of the "Progression Standard" (usually 20-30 reps for Step 1, 5-10 for advanced steps).

---

## 1.3 THE TEMPO PRINCIPLE (Wade, Sommer)

### The Core Law:
**Controlled tempo builds tendons. Speed builds nothing.**

- **The 2-1-2 Tempo:** 2 seconds down, 1 second pause, 2 seconds up.
- **Sommer's Steady State:** Tendons adapt 10x slower than muscles. SLOW progression is mandatory.

### The Fundamental Practice:
1.  **No Bouncing:** Dead starts only. Relax at bottom, then explode.
2.  **No Momentum:** Every rep is deliberate.

---

## 1.4 THE BIG 6 (Wade)

### The Core Law:
**6 movements = Complete body.**

1.  **Pushup** (Chest, Triceps, Shoulders)
2.  **Squat** (Legs, Glutes)
3.  **Pullup** (Back, Biceps)
4.  **Leg Raise** (Abs, Hip Flexors)
5.  **Bridge** (Spine, Posterior Chain)
6.  **Handstand Pushup** (Shoulders, Traps)

### The Fundamental Practice:
- **Fewer is Better:** Do NOT add more exercises. Master these 6.

# PART 2: LEARNING FUNDAMENTALS

## 2.1 THE ENCODING PRINCIPLE (Bremer, Clear)

### The Core Law:
**Information must be ENCODED, not just read.**

- **Bremer's USM Algorithm:**
  1.  **Filter:** Identify the 20% that matters.
  2.  **Mental File:** Organize into categories.
  3.  **Alpha State:** Relax before encoding (4-4-4 breathing).
  4.  **Recursive Review:** Test yourself, don't re-read.

### The Fundamental Practice:
1.  **Close the Book:** After reading, write 3 bullet points from memory.
2.  **If you can't, you didn't learn it.**

---

## 2.2 THE TESTING EFFECT (Accelerated Learning)

### The Core Law:
**Retrieval > Recognition.**

- Re-reading creates the ILLUSION of competence.
- Testing creates ACTUAL competence.

### The Fundamental Practice:
1.  **Test, Don't Re-Read:** Use flashcards, practice problems, or self-quizzing.
2.  **Spacing:** Test again after 1 day, 1 week, 1 month.

---

## 2.3 THE 2-MINUTE RULE (Clear)

### The Core Law:
**The first 2 minutes are the Momentum Gate.**

- Starting is 80% of the battle.
- **Clear's Rule:** "When you start a new habit, it should take less than 2 minutes to do."

### The Fundamental Practice:
1.  Set a 2-minute timer.
2.  Just START (open the book, do 1 pushup, write 1 sentence).
3.  After 2 minutes, you're legally allowed to stop—but you won't.

# PART 3: PSYCHOLOGY FUNDAMENTALS

## 3.1 THE IDENTITY PRINCIPLE (Clear)

### The Core Law:
**Behavior follows identity.**

- **Clear's Law:** "Every action is a vote for the type of person you wish to become."
- Don't say "I want to be strong." Say "I AM a strong person."

### The Fundamental Practice:
1.  **Identity Statement:** Write "I am [X]" and act accordingly.
2.  **Proof Stack:** Every action that aligns with the identity is proof.

# PART 5: TACTICAL FUNDAMENTALS

## 5.1 THE AWARENESS PRINCIPLE (KGB, Musashi)

### The Core Law:
**Situational awareness > Physical strength.**

- **KGB Teaching:** "The best fight is the one you avoid."
- **Musashi:** "Perceive that which cannot be seen."

### The Fundamental Practice:
1.  **Scan Every Room:** Exits, threats, allies.
2.  **Trust Your Gut:** If it feels wrong, it IS wrong.

---

## 5.2 THE SIMPLICITY PRINCIPLE (All Sources)

### The Core Law:
**Complexity is the enemy of execution.**

- **Pavel:** "Grease the Groove" (GTG) = Practice 1 move, many times/day.
- **Wade:** "The Big 6" = 6 moves, not 60.
- **Clear:** "2-Minute Rule" = Start small, build momentum.

### The Fundamental Practice:
1.  **Pick ONE thing.**
2.  **Do it every day.**
3.  **Master it before adding more.**
