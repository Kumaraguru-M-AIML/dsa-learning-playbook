## 2. THE RATE OF ADAPTATION (ARE YOU STALLING?)
*   **The Beginner Curve (Weeks 1-12):** You should get stronger *every single session*.
    *   *Mechanism:* Neural Learning.
    *   *Expected Gain:* +2.5% to +5% per week.
*   **The Intermediate Plateaus (Months 6-12):** Strength gains slow to weekly/monthly.
*   **The Stall Signal:** If you do not hit a PR (Rep or Weight) for **2 consecutive weeks** in Phase 0, you are either:
    1.  Under-recovering (Sleep/Nutrition).
    2.  Over-training (Too much "junk volume").

## 3. PUSHING STABILITY: THE RING STANDARD
Why "Ring Pushups" for Apex?
*   **Planar Stability:** A floor pushup is stable. The ground doesn't move.
*   **Ring Pushup:** The rings want to move in 360 degrees. Your small stabilizer muscles (Rotator Cuff, Serratus Anterior) must fire 10x harder to keep them still.
*   **The Metric:** If you shake uncontrollably on rings, your "Prime Movers" (Pecs) are strong, but your "Stabilizers" are weak. This is **Fake Strength**.

## 4. DEADLIFT CAPACITY (WITHOUT A BARBELL)
How to measure Hinge Strength at home?
*   **The Single-Leg RDL:** It tests Hinge Strength + Balance + Symmetry.
*   **The Standard:** Can you hinge at the hip until your torso is parallel to the floor, holding a weight in the *opposite* hand, without your knee caving in?
*   **Apex Level:** Doing this with 50% Bodyweight in hand.

## 5. PAIN AS A METRIC
*   **Muscle Soreness (DOMS):** Acceptable (Day 1-2).
*   **Joint Pain:** UNACCEPTABLE.
    *   *Sharp Pain:* Stop immediately. Structural failure.
    *   *Dull Ache:* Inflammation. Check technique.
*   **The Phantom Rule:** "Pain-Free Performance." If it hurts the joint, you aren't strong enough to do it yet. Regress.
