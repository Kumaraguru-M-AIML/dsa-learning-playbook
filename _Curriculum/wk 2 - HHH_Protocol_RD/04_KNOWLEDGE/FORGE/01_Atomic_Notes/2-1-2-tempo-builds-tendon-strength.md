# Slow tempo (2-1-2) builds tendon strength that outlasts muscle growth

---

## 📌 THE CORE IDEA
The 2-1-2 tempo means: 2 seconds down (eccentric), 1 second pause, 2 seconds up (concentric). At this speed, the tendons and connective tissue receive time-under-tension that causes them to adapt and thicken. Muscle grows in days. Tendons grow in months. If you skip tendon conditioning, your muscles will outpace your joints — and you will get injured the moment you load them seriously.

---

## 🧬 WHY IT MATTERS (So What?)
Most people move too fast through reps (1 second down, throw up). This trains muscle but bypasses tendon. In 6-12 months, their muscle is strong but their joints are fragile. The 2-1-2 tempo is the insurance policy that lets you train at high intensity for *years* without injury. This is why CC practitioners at Step 3 are more durable than gym beginners at "advanced" exercises.

---

## ⚡ WHEN TO USE THIS
*"I reach for this when..."*
- Every single rep of every exercise — this is not occasional, it is the default
- Especially critical: when starting a new exercise or a new step in a progression
- When feeling impatient and tempted to rush reps — this is exactly when tendons get skipped
- When diagnosing why someone got injured: first question is always "what was their tempo?"
