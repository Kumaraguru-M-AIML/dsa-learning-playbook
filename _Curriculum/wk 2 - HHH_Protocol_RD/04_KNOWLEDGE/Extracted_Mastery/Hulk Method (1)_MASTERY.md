R e s t   a n d   r e c o v e r y

Building  muscle,  on  the  face  of  it,  is
really  quite  simple.  You  need  to
create  a  stimulus  for  your  muscles  to
grow.

When you lift weights, you’re breaking
your muscle fibers down, so they can
grow  back  bigger  and  stronger.  By

challenging your muscles to deal
with greater levels of resistance and
weight, your muscles will increase
in size, also known as muscle
hyper trophy.
02
N u t r i t i o n

When you’re trying to build muscle,
your diet is just as important as when
you want to lose weight. Eat the right
amounts of carbs, fats, and in particular
protein — which helps repair damaged
muscle fibers.

If you don’t provide your body with the

food it needs to perform, you won’t see
the gains you’re looking for — it’s that
simple…

Sometimes less is more. When you
lift weights, you’re essentially tearing
muscle fibers in order to make way for
new muscle growth. And, this muscle
tissue actually grows back when you’re
not training!

So, although you might be tempted to
train a muscle group more often, you
might not see the results you want.
Turn those rest days into growth days.
04

T h e   f i n a l   w o r d

The only way to build muscle is with
proper training, good nutrition, and
not forgetting rest. You must apply
equal attention to all three to maximize
muscle growth.

And, remember…

You can’t out-train a bad diet.

A drop set is a training technique
where you perform a normal set, but
rather than heading straight into your
rest, you drop the weight and perform
another set until failure.

They can push your muscles beyond
their normal limits, increasing the
time they’re under tension, as well as
the training volume, which can ignite
muscle growth.

It’s a versatile technique that can be
used during any workout — from legs
to abs.
