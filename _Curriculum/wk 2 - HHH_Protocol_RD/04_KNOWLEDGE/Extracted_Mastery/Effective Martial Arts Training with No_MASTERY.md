Introduction:

The goal of martial arts is simply personal development.  The main point of
martial  arts  training  is  to  develop  is  maturity,  so  even  though  this  book  is
based around martial arts, the principles and techniques can be applied to any
constructive  activity  in  which  you  want  to  achieve  greatness  and  success.
Success creates maturity, and maturity creates success. My understanding of
maturity  is  as  follows:  Control  over  your  mind  and  your  mental  focus;
solving  problems  through  logic;  controlling  and  conquering  your  feelings;
being  honest,  humble,  and  genuine  towards  yourself  and  others;  being  very
emotionally and spiritually motivated to reach your goals; having a great deal
of  self-discipline;  being  genuinely  curious  and  adventurous  towards  life;
being able to express yourself fully and wholeheartedly at all times with no
social  restraints;  being  able  to  become,  in  an  instant,  violent  and  brutal  if
needed and switching it off in a second if needed; and choosing a career-path
that is fun and gives you great joy.

Kia and inner power:

A  kia  is  a  forceful  type  of  yelling  or  screaming  that  is  done  along  with  a
strike. The primary purpose of a kia is to increase the force of a blow; think
of  it  as  a  fully  committed  expression  of  inner  intensity  or  power.  A  kia  is
should be as intense as possible, releasing all of your potential and power in
one instance.  Besides the obvious constructiveness in perhaps being able to
finish a violent confrontation with one blow, there is also a spiritual side to
kia. By doing kias, you tap into your inner primordial force. Think of the way
animals attack; every man and woman is born with this force inside of them.
By  tapping  into  it  we  learn  to  control  it,  and  we  get  a  sort  of  balance  and
tranquility  in  our  being.  When  you  accomplish  this,  there  is  no  part  of  you
that  is  suppressed.  Almost  everyone  has  extreme  difficulty  doing  kias  in
practice when they first begin their training. Anger in our society is frowned
upon,  and  is  largely  taboo.  Also,  people  have  trouble  seemingly  losing
control in front of others and going “crazy.” This is a symptom of them not
being  able  to  fully  express  themselves  in  general.  They  might  have  trouble
saying  no  to  someone  else,  or  truly  speaking  their  mind.  Something  is
holding them back. They are most likely frustrated within themselves.

This way of thought might sound a bit “new age” to beginners, but by doing
kias  you  can  achieve  a  sort  of  inner  balance  and  calmness,  and  shed  the
expectations  of  others  along  with  the  rules  of  what  you  “should”  be  doing
with  your  life.  By  tapping  into  this  force  you  become  a  mentally  strong
human being. You can then use this strength to increase your ability to be “in
the  moment,”  or  to  be  100%  aware  and  committed  to  the  moment.  This
ability  will  help  you  get  results  quicker  in  whatever  you  might  be  doing.  If
you don’t seem to be in the moment in what you are doing, if something feels
wrong, then maybe you shouldn’t be where you are and doing what you are
doing. Always feel within yourself, as this is where the key to your happiness
lies.

To  be  able  to  work  with  yourself  you  have  to  be  able  to  listen  to  yourself.
Most people have gotten a lot of rules from their parents and society. This is
something  you  have  ingested  without  asking  questions  and  at  an  age  where
you may not have had the ability to ask questions. Learn to become aware of

these things; be aware of these roles and how they are controlling your life.
Are  your  opinions  your  own?  Feel  within  yourself  and  try  to  answer  these
questions.  It  can  be  very  painful  and  uncomfortable,  but  try  to  forget  your
ego. If you want to change something in your life you have to know: What
are the reasons for what you are experiencing. Why are you not happy? Why
is the road you are walking not working out? Which road should you be on?

You  can  practice  these  forceful  screams  by  yourself.  Try  to  focus  on
completely letting go, and express your rage and intensity fully. Think of it as
an opera singer expressing their entire being in a scream. Try to do these in a
forest or some other place where you are completely alone, because if other
people  hear  you  screaming  like  this  they  might  grow  very  concerned  and
disrupt your training.

Meditation:

It’s  easy  to  get  confused  about  what  meditation  really  is.  There  are  a  lot  of
styles  and  schools  of  meditation,  and  through  this  jungle  it’s  very  hard  to
pinpoint exactly what this whole phenomenon is and where exactly to begin.

First of all: Meditation is not supposed to be complicated. It’s not a science.
Meditation is a method to calm yourself, and essentially remove the mind for
a period of time. The untrained mind works as a sponge; its basic method is
explicit  association.  Your  mind  constantly  bombards  you  with  needless
impulses, information, and memories. Your mind is constantly analyzing, and
words are flying through your head. Some refer this state of the (untrained)
mind  as  “monkey  brain.”  Meditation  is  a  tool  you  can  use  to  escape  and
control the monkey brain.

Meditation has two main purposes:

The first purpose of meditation is to keep your mind calm at all times. This is
beneficial in a number of ways: You spend less energy on needless stuff. You
seem to have time and energy for other people. You have the energy to meet
other people genuinely and listen to them with all your being. You develop
the  ability  to  focus  on  something  exclusively  for  a  period  of  time  and
therefore get better result in that field more quickly. Your awareness of your
surroundings  and  the  moment  increases  because  you’re  not  wasting  your
mind’s energy thinking about unimportant things.

Focus  in  the  moment  is  absolutely  crucial  in  martial  arts.  At  the  simplest
level, you get hit if you lose focus. If you lose focus and start to tense up and
make  tactical  and  technical  mistakes,  you  are  on  the  short  road  to  defeat.
Using  your  calm  mind  to  genuinely  interact  with  other  people  and  by  truly
being  in  the  moment  while  you  interact  with  them  teaches  you  to  know
people and how they react. You learn to read them and respond to them in a
constructive  way.  Besides  being  a  great  life  skill,  being  able  to  read  your
opponent  is  also  a  crucial  skill  to  have  in  martial  arts.  Being  able  to  focus
wholeheartedly  while  studying  or  teaching  a  subject  increases  learning

dramatically.  Being  intuitionally  aware  of  your  surroundings  is  also  very
important in a combat situation as well as in your day-to-day life.

The second purpose of meditation is that once you reach this “no-mind” state
through  meditation,  you  can  use  this  incredible  focus  and  calmness  to  do
powerful  things.  For  example,  you  can  visualize  your  goals.  You  can
visualize  yourself  performing  actions  that  directly  fulfill  your  goals,  for
instance, passing a belt test. You can visualize yourself being successful and
winning. Imagine this as a small movie playing in your head with you as the
main character. But your entire being has to be in this movie in order for this
technique to become very powerful. You have to truly think, believe, and feel
this movie as if it was reality. Your mind should be without a shred of doubt.
Your subconscious will then respond to this as if it was reality, and you will
begin to perform the actions that will facilitate and create the results you are
visualizing.  Just  remember  to  give  yourself  a  chance  to  be  able  to  perform
these  actions,  such  as  going  to  the  gym,  the  dojo,  or  hitting  the  pavement.
Seek out the place or information that will enable you to perform the actions
that you need to do to make your mental movie come true.

Another  thing  that  is  worth  mentioning  is  that  to  increase  the  true
believability in your being while visualizing, this should be something that is
very realistic for you to achieve, and you should start with something smaller
than the end goal. This will minimize the chance of you starting to doubt your
goal, even if it’s just a microsecond of doubt. For instance, if you want to be
a black belt, take it one belt test at a time. When you have achieved one goal,
then you can start focusing on the next one. Stick with one or two goals in
your  life  and  break  them  down  into  something  that  can  be  achieved  in  six
months  to  a  year’s  time.  Write  those  goals  down.  If  you  are  really  serious,
those  two  goals  are  all  you  should  be  focusing  on  in  your  life.  Block  out
anything  or  anyone  else  who  won’t  help  you  achieve  those  goals,  and  then
the results will come fast. It should literally be the first thing on your mind in
the  morning  and  the  last  thing  on  your  mind  in  the  evening  before  you  fall
asleep. Nothing can add more power to your life than concentrating all your
energies on a limited set of targets.

You  can  also  use  this  focused  state  of  mind  to  detect  emotions  deep  within
you that you might be suppressing in your normal day to day life. Run your

failures through your mind when it’s in this state and see what you feel. Not
what you think, but what you feel. What do you see? Not words popping up;
you  might  come  to  some  understanding  that  can  take  days  before  you  can
articulate it. Things like your happiness, what it is you really want to do in
life, all kinds of addictions and fears and dreams; these are some of the things
you  should  work  on  while  in  this  state  of  mind.  A  great  question  to  ask
yourself is: Are my habits, actions and behaviors congruent with the vision I
have for my life?

Working  with  and  correcting  some  of  these  larger  life  issues  should  make
you a more fulfilled and happy person. It should give you confidence and a
real  purpose  in  life.  You  should  be  able  to  express  yourself  to  the  utmost.
This relates very much to your life in general as well as to your martial arts
training. In essence, martial arts training is learning to express your inner self
to the fullest, and by doing so, growing as a person. Remove all restrictions,
all negative emotions, everyone else’s expectations, and just be you and live
your life.

So how exactly do you meditate?

Find  a  place  with  few  or  no  people  and  little  noise.  Make  sure  it’s  a  place
where  you  feel  comfortable.  You  can  meditate  standing  up,  lying  down,  or
sitting.  If  you  decide  to  sit  down,  make  sure  your  back  is  straight,  but  not
tensed  up.  Your  body  should  be  relaxed,  same  as  your  mind.  If  you  have  a
hard time relaxing try to go through your body relaxing one area at the time.
Start with your feet and work your way up. The last thing you relax is your
mind.  But  remember,  your  mind  will  fight  this;  random  thoughts  will  pop
into  your  mind.  One  way  to  avoid  this  is  to  focus  on  your  breathing.  You
should perform long, natural, deep breaths. Make sure they reach all the way
down into your stomach and that you completely empty your lungs each time
without  force.  Always  remember  to  relax.  But  don’t  THINK  about  your
breathing- just observe and focus. The key is not to have explicit thoughts. A
common beginner’s error is wanting to let go, and thinking about letting go.
You  have  to  let  go  of  this  as  well.  You  must  actually  “lose”  your  mind.  If
thoughts  come  to  you,  avoid  the  temptation  of  engaging  and  starting  to
analyze them. Just observe and let them naturally pass. Don’t get frustrated,
but instead be patient and calm.

is  sometimes  referred  to  as  working  or  walking  meditation.  When  you  are
doing a task, however mundane it may seem, try to relax your mind and focus
on  what  you’re  doing.  Don’t  multitask  and  plan  ahead  in  your  head.  If  you
are  preoccupied  with  something  else,  the  work  you  do  will  become  sloppy,

Negative thoughts:

Through this meditation training you should be able to calm your mind and
therefore  have  less  impulsive  and  fragmented  association-type  thoughts.
Once  this  happens,  you  will  have  the  clarity  to  start  noticing  more  deeply
exactly the type of thoughts you are experiencing. The obvious goal is to get
rid of any kind of negative and destructive thoughts and thoughts-patterns. If
you  find  yourself  in  a  situation  where  you  start  to  doubt  your  ability  to
achieve  something,  or  if  thinking  about  a  past  situation  or  perhaps  an
argument  fills  you  with  anger,  frustration,  hate,  envy,  or  something  similar,
then  stop  and  reflect  upon  this.  Ask  yourself:  Why  am  I  thinking  like  this?
What is the cause of my frustration? Is it something in my environment that
is the cause of this? What is triggering this? If it’s some sort of activity that
triggers these negative thoughts, then stop doing that activity immediately! If
it’s  a  person,  then  cut  all  ties  with  that  person  as  soon  as  possible!  You
become what you think. Make a dedicated, conscious effort to think and talk
to  yourself  with  positive  messages  and  expectations  of  success.  Coach
yourself  to  be  committed,  and  use  your  emotional  drives.  Expect  success
from yourself, and really believe in it. You’ll automatically work towards in
the  real  world  what  consumes  your  inner  world.  Don’t  give  up  on  your
happiness  with  hopelessness.  Your  dominant  thoughts  of  the  day  should
always be on your vision for the future. Not the past, but the future that you
create. Your future is a product of your actions today.

I’ll give you a few examples from my own life: If you’re really serious about
martial arts and really want to better yourself through it, there are some things
that  ordinary  people  do  that  you  have  to  give  up.  The  most  obvious  are
alcohol,  cigarettes,  and  junk  food.  Usually  these  things  are  tied  to  a  social
context,  so  you  have  to  remove  yourself  from  that  social  context  if  you  are
serious about this new life path. I’m not only talking about a physical place,
but  even  more  so  about  the  people  that  inhabit  those  places.  You  have  to
stand your ground and tell your friends that you can’t be that same friend that
they are used to. You have to change and become someone else. This is tough
and  takes  a  lot  of  maturity  from  you.  Most  people  don’t  like  to  disappoint
others, and they shy away from confrontation and conflict because they seek

the approval of others. This may seem beneficial in the short run, but in the
long run your dreams will slip away from you and never be obtained, simply
because you didn’t have the maturity to act on them.

When I stopped drinking and the other negative things that go along with a
life  of  alcohol,  my  friends  were,  on  the  surface,  understanding  in  the
beginning. But it was just a front. If we take a look through history, we see
that  people  have  a  very  hard  time  coping  with  change.  For  instance,  if
politicians  try  to  change  a  society,  they  often  don’t  get  reelected.  Another
example is that people often shy away from trying new things because they
are afraid of failure. This is, of course, very non-productive because it’s when
we  fail  that  we  learn  and  evolve.  When  their  drinking  buddy  started  to
change, my so-called friends became frustrated and turned that anger towards
me.  The  idea  was  to  punish  this  new  behavior  and  bring  me  back  as  the
person they had come to know, and I was not mature enough at that time to
speak  up  and  walk  away  when  the  first  little  signs  started  to  appear.  But
eventually,  I  grew  tired  and  went  my  own  way.  So  don’t  wait,  just  ignore
them and walk away. Don’t even hold a grudge or feel hate if you have been
wronged; simply walk away. Don’t spend your time and energy on negative
people;  people  who  try  to  belittle  you,  who  criticize  and  attack  you  as  a
person.    And  more  importantly:  Don’t  be  that  guy  yourself!  Never  belittle
others. If you catch yourself doing it, then reflect upon why you are doing it
and then change the reason. Usually it stems from your own insecurity. For
example,  in  the  case  of  my  friends,  they  didn’t  feel  that  they  were  strong
enough  to  make  it  on  their  own  or  to  make  new  friends  now  that  I  had
changed.  Always
to  be  honest,  open,  humble,  and
straightforward  towards  others.  If  you  are  insecure,  it’s  often  because  of
failures from your past. If that’s the case, then dedicate yourself to something
that you would deem as very successful for you if you achieve it. If you are
reading this book, martial arts would most certainly be one of those things.

Emotional motivator:

You  need  to  develop  a  personal  emotional-based  motivator  for  when  the
going  gets  tough.  Martial  arts  at  a  high  level  involves  at  lot  of  boring  and
repetitious drills and conditioning. Martial arts also contains a lot of sparring
and  tests  that  can  be  very  painful  and  uncomfortable.  You  need  to  push
through this and ignore it when the brain tells you to quit. The brains role in
this is self-preservation. The brain operates on short-term logic and you need
a  motivator  that  is  stronger  than  that;  a  motivator  that  is  based  on  emotion
and your personal convictions; a motivator that’s internal. When you discover
your convictions, you discover your passions. Fulfilling these convictions and
passions should be your life mission. The result is happiness if you possess
the bravery to go after it with everything you have.

To develop this motivation, you need to be brutally honest with yourself. Ask
yourself the question: Why do you want this? What are you trying to prove to
yourself? What do you want to learn? Who do you want to be? And you need
to answer these questions in terms of feelings. What kind of emotional energy
is  driving  this  pursuit?  What  is  the  strongest  emotional  drive  to  pursue  this
goal? It could be someone close to you who told you that you would fail in
life and that everything about you was wrong. It could be a value that you are
willing  to  die  for.  It  could  be  a  desire  to  care  for  your  loved  ones.  Find  a
mental  image  and/or  phrase  that  will  instantly  put  you  back  in  the  state  of
whatever is emotional driving your goal. Use this phrase or image when you
need it; when things get really hard and you need to push through. Personally,
my emotional motivator is that I want to be great. I want to be a great man.
And when that happens, I want to make others great. My phrase is, “I want to
be great.” My mental image is of people from where I grew up who told me
that  I  would  never  be  great;  and  that  essentially  that  I  would  end  up  like
them.

An  important  thing  to  remember  it  that  this  image  or  phrase  is  used  for
motivation, not problem solving; problem solving should always be based on
logic.  You  need  to  be  able  to  master  and  use  both  systems  simultaneously.
For instance: which techniques to use against an opponent should be based on
logic (experience, knowledge, etc.), But the will to actually take a chance and

attack him, especially if you’re tired, should be based on emotional energy.

Adversity:

Adversity  is  the  primary  ingredient  in  mental  toughness.  To  put  it  another
way, adversity is the only place that mental toughness can be created. It’s like
a factory, this is the only place where they produce this product that we are
after: mental toughness. So if you want to be mentally tough and succeed in
your goals you need to seek out and create adversity. Push yourself, with the
use  of  the  emotional  motivator,  to  exhaustion.  Removing  the  struggle
removes the victory. Afterwards, you can relax.

Think about 3-5 times where you’ve faced adversity, and then think about the
consequences. Where there any long-term negative effects of the adversity?
Were there any positive long-term effects of the experiences? Do you regret
today what you went through in the past? Hopefully you will find that even
though it was very uncomfortable at the time, today you look back with some
kind of pride and satisfaction of doing something that most people couldn’t,
and work to create more episodes like that.

Ideas for creating adversity:

Over-train for a few days. Train outside in the cold with no shoes and little
clothing.  Do  something  that  you  really  suck  at  in  front  of  everybody.  Let
someone  beat  you  up  in  sparring  and  then  continue  fighting  and  training.
Don’t sleep for a few days and train like hell instead. Skip meals and train on
an empty stomach.

Mapping and planning:

You need to know with the utmost clarity what you want, why you want it,
and  how  to  achieve  it.  Always  know  the  answers  to  every  one  of  those
questions.  Update  and  change  this  plan  all  the  time.  Always  have  it  and  its
contents in your mind. Be obsessed with it and its contents.  Review it daily.
Being able to do this requires open-mindedness and humbleness. You need to
be able give up everything you think you know and change in an instant. Rid
yourself  of  prejudices  and  old  ideas.  If  you  discover  that  something  can  be
done  better  or  faster,  adapt  and  change  immediately.  Always  try  to  gain  an
edge and find a better way. Always be open, flexible, and innovative. If you
read something, try it out and then use it if it’s better than what you used to
do. When it comes to new ideas, check your ego at the door and suspend your
disbelief, then evaluate the facts. Also, think for yourself, and if you have an
idea try it out and see what happens. Take risks and be bold. Maybe you’ll
discover something new, or maybe now you’ll know what you should avoid
doing. Avoid sayings like: “this is fact,” or “this is the truth,” or “this is the
only way,” because maybe it’s not. Remember that all your goals, both short-
term and long-term, started as ideas.

What/Who do you want to become:

Have  long-term  goals  and  short-term  goals.  Meditate  and  visualize  on  the
short-term ones. The long-term ones should always revolve around trying to
be  the  best  you  can  be.  Winning  is  being  better  today  than  you  were
yesterday. You dreams and goals are not what you do; they’re what you are.
Success and winning is the fullest expression of yourself and your potential.
Winning  is  fulfilling  your  potential.  The  field  you  choose  is  the  thing  you
love  the  most,  so  make  sure  you  think  it  is  fun.  This  is  your  vision,  your
mental  picture  of  you  in  the  future,  your  version  of  the  perfect  life.  Try
asking yourself: if there were no limits what would I be, have, and do?

Why:

This  is  the  emotional  motivator;  this  is  very  important.  This  is  the  passion
and  conviction  that  your  actions  flow  from.  This  is  the  self-discipline  and
determination. Why do you want to be great, and why this field(s)? You have
to know why you are fighting. Ask yourself: Why must I suffer? The answer
is the emotional motivator.

How:

Study the subject. Always be curious and creative. Talk to people who have
done what you want to do. Adopt a beginner’s mindset even if you’re not a
beginner. Read this series. Create a team around you that will help you reach
your goals. Create great allies and mentors wherever you go, and don’t just
receive from them. Help and support them genuinely as well. Always be very
humble towards them. Never be arrogant towards others. Share the accolades
and  the  victory.  You’ll  get  that  energy  back  tenfold,  and  therefore  succeed
faster. You can’t do it alone. Always look for that little edge and be creative
with what you learn. A solution or principle might work in a different field
than the one from which it originated. Experiment, take risks, and see what
happens. But remember that often, less is more, meaning that the more simple
solution or principle is often better than the complex one. Be very conscious
of  what  you  have  to  do  to  attain  your  long-term  goal  and  the  activities  that
create the desired results. Make doing these things a habit. Know when and
how to train, what to eat, when to go to bed, etc. Write it down, and read and
think about these things every day and make them your habits. Follow your
program  to  the  tee  so  that  it  quickly  becomes  habit.  This  frees  up  mental
energy you can then use to learn new thing to incorporate into your program,
and tweak and change your habits so you achieve your goals more quickly. A
habit usually takes 3-4 weeks to really kick in. You should always approach
the  “how”  with  a  sense  of  urgency.  As  soon  as  you  have  even  the  slightest
general  idea  of  how  to  accomplish  your  goals,  then  move  forward  without
fear.  You  never  know  how  much  time  you  have  left.  If  you  have  trouble
motivating yourself to action, use your emotional motivator. Later the actions
will become habits and thus require less motivational energy.

But don’t fill your head with needless and shallow information. The point of
information  is  to  seek  greater  competence  through  knowledge.  Information
and knowledge is not the same thing. Always remember that.

Fear of failure doesn’t exist because you cannot
lose:

This might sound a bit foreign for some of you. For instance, when you have
truly adopted the mindset of personal development as the goal of martial arts,
then  the  existence  of  fear,  and  therefore  motivation,  goes  away.  Let  me
explain: when we feel fear it’s because we perceive the possibility of failure
to  be  imminent,  and  we  believe  failure  to  be  harmful.  If  you’re  competing
against other people and you don’t beat them, then you experience feelings of
failure; you feel that you lose face, you feel like you’re smaller and less than
that  other  individual.  Those  negative  feelings  will  create  fear,  and  lead  to
diminished self-worth and kill motivation for constructive actions. This is a
mindset we need to avoid at all costs.

The  mindset  that  we  need  to  create  is  one  of  us  as  competing  against  our
selves. In this mindset, there is no difference or distinction between success
and  failures.  There  are  only  experiences  that  better  you.  You  can’t  lose.  If
you  are  training  and  you  succeed,  then  it’s  something  good.  Let’s  say  you
accomplish  one  of  your  short  term  goals,  then  that  is  a  success  and  is  only
positive. If you “fail” a short-term goal, the only consequence of that is that
you learn that whatever you were doing wasn’t good enough. You gain a very
constructive  and  important  fact;  you  gain  the  knowledge  and  truth  that  you
need  to  have  in  order  to  achieve  your  goals.  Without  this  knowledge,  you
can’t  reach  your  goals.  So  for  us  to  gain  our  long-term  goal  (personal
development/learning and growing) we have to fail in the short-term. A wise
man sees failure as progress. The more we fail, the more we know. This is the
“how” of the plan. Forget all immature thoughts of instant gratification, delay
your  gratification,  and  reap  the  massive  rewards  and  payoff  years  in  the
future. So try everything out that might be helpful somehow. Take risks and
be bold. Find out what works for you. The more you are willing to fail in the
short-term, the faster you’ll accomplish your long-term goals. Always think
about  and  learn  from  your  failures.  Have  a  humble,  logic-based,  problem-
solving  approach  towards  your  failures.  You  should  be  grateful  for  and
cherish  your  failures  both  in  the  past  and  future.  You,  in  essence,  fail  your
way  to  success.  Mediate  on  this  understanding  daily  and  make  a  choice  to

adopt this mindset. Make a choice to start monitoring your thoughts, and alter
them as needed towards the thoughts discussed in this book. Also remember
that the more you fail, the easier failing becomes; the less discomfort you’ll
feel, until one day it doesn’t bother you at all in a negative emotional way. In
fact, what usually happens is that the joy of conquering your fears and having
success  in  the  activity  will  create  a  great  love  for  the  activity.  You  become
addicted  to  the  joy  of  success  (i.e.  growing  as  a  human  being)  and  do
everything  in  your  power  to  experience  more  of  it,  so  the  fear  goes  away.
This  genuine  and  intrinsic  joy-based  motivation  is  off  course  extremely
valuable  and  positive,  both  in  terms  of  you  achieving  your  goals  in  martial
arts  (or  something  else)  and  in  terms  of  your  general  well-being  and
happiness. You’ll know when you get there by the fact that you’ll focus on
and  get  joy  from  the  activity  itself,  and  not  from  the  results  of  the  activity.
The  funny  thing  is  that  when  you  focus  on  the  activity  in  itself  rather  than
focusing on the results, the results will actually come quicker.

Building a network:

As  I  mentioned  earlier,  when  you  want  to  achieve  something  massive  and
gain personal development in the process, you need to both isolate yourself
and be very social. I’ll talk a little about this somewhat contradictory fact. To
put  it  in  very  simple  terms,  you  need  to  rid  yourself  of  negative,
unconstructive  people,  and  gain  the  trust  and  friendship  of  positive  and
constructive  people.  This  is  actually  a  lot  more  important  than  most  people
think. No matter who you are you’re going to need help in your quest. Let’s
use martial arts as an example:

People  you  need  to  develop  relationships  with:  Trainers,  training  partners,
people to relax and unwind with, fitness experts, mature mentors, and people
who are knowledgeable about meditation, motivation, and success.

People  you  need  to  avoid:  Mean-spirited  people:  People  who  don’t  respect
you, who doesn’t listen to you, who try to change you, spiritually lazy people.

Remember  that  every  healthy  friendship  is  based  on  balance  and  equality.
Don’t  use  other  people  to  reach  your  goals.  Help  them  to  reach  them  with
you. Drag them up with you and let yourself be dragged up by them. Share
your knowledge and experiences with them. Always listen to them and look
them  in  the  eyes  with  a  kind  and  observant  attitude.  Learn  some
conversational  skills.  Also  remember  to  stay  in  touch  with  the  good  people
you meet in your life; if you don’t they’ll slip away from you. Another thing
to remember is that you attract people on your own maturity level. So if you
only meet negative people in your life, look inwards and make the change in
yourself and your life. If you do this wholeheartedly then you’ll notice that
you start to meet positive people and you’ll automatically push the negative
people away. When the student is ready the master(s) will appear.

Opposites and balance:

The concept of opposites and balance is important to understand in regards to
martial  arts  and  the  principle  of  maturity.  Every  concept  has  two  extreme
opposite  poles.  These  poles  are  often  referred  to  as  hard  and  soft.  For
instance,  we  have  talked  about  the  concept  of  inner  force  in  this  book.  The
hard  pole  of  inner  force  is  kia:  The  expression  of  all  of  your  inner  power
expressed  as  aggression  in  a  single  strike.  The  soft  and  opposite  pole  is
meditation: Calmness and inner focus. The concept of opposites is important
to understand because you need to realize that if you master both poles and
their expression, you master everything in between; you master balance. And
that’s the goal. So when you train at something, treat it as an extreme. Go as
far out towards one pole as you can and you will learn more quickly.

Since I defined balance as the goal, let me speak some more on this. Balance
is control over the situation. Every situation is different, and the situation you
are  in  changes  constantly.  You  therefore  have  to  adapt  to  the  situation
constantly, and you have to constantly manipulate and change the situation to
fit  your  needs  and  objective.  To  be  able  to  do  this,  you  need  to  master  the
extremes of a concept, as well as anything in between. Usually we talk about
the physical, mental, and spiritual realms of a subject or activity. Off course,
this  way  of  putting  it  is  a  bit  too  shallow,  but  the  point  is  for  you  to
understand  the  basic  idea.  The  hard  physical  pole  is  impact:  Hard  sparring,
heavybag, strength training, and hard body training. The soft physical pole is
deep  breathing  and  stretching.  The  hard  side  of  the  mental  aspect  is  the
emotional  motivator  and  the  logic-based  approach  towards  problems.  The
soft side is the intrinsic love and joy you have towards life, the activity, and
your lifestyle. The soft sides of all 3 concepts tend to blend together as do the
hard sides.

Let me illustrate the idea of using balance to make a situation as positive and
constructive as possible. If someone attacks you in the street, you would off
course  use  the  hard  poles.  If  a  person  needed  guidance,  cheering  up,  and
lacked  energy  and  motivation  towards  life  you  would  use  the  soft  poles  to
turn  the  situation  into  something  positive.  These  are  the  extremes  and
examples of how you would use them in real life.  In almost all situations you

should  use  balance.  For  instance,  if  someone  comes  to  you  with  something
about you that they don’t feel is very positive, you have to use a logic-based
problem solving approach; you also have to be humble to see if the individual
has  a  legitimate  point.  You  also  need  to  be  able  to  draw  a  line  if  the
individual  crosses  the  line  and  attacks  you  personally.  Just  slightly  raising
your  voice  or  changing  the  look  in  your  eyes  should  be  more  than  enough.
You  need  to  be  able  to  weave  together  all  of  these  things  and  change  the
intensity  of  the  techniques  instantly  as  the  situation  changes  and  develops.
This is my understanding of the concepts of balance and yin and yang.

Breathing:

We have touched on breathing before in regards to meditation and flexibility
(Volume  2  of  this  series).  In  essence,  there  are  two  main  different  ways  of
breathing:  A  long,  deep,  and  relaxed  method,  and  a  forceful  method.  The
forceful  kind  is  usually  divided  into  a  short  burst  and  a  longer  forceful
exhaling method of breathing.

Functional breathing:

This  type  of  breathing  is  used  when  you  are  hitting  a  target  with  a  part  of
your body (takedown, strike etc.). If you are pushing away from yourself, you
should exhale in a quick burst. It shouldn’t be a violent burst, but an assertive
burst. This should be done for a number of reasons. First of all, it increases
the  intensity  of  the  technique.  Secondly,  it  empties  your  lungs  somewhat
when  you  are  close  to  your  opponent  when  the  risk  of  being  countered  and
taking a hit to the body is imminent. Having little air in your lungs at the time
of  impact  greatly  decreases  the  effectiveness  of  the  strike  against  you.  The
third  thing  is  that  it  makes  sure  that  you  will  get  new  air  and  energy  when
you sidestep away from your opponent after delivering your combination for
instance.  If  the  lungs  are  emptied,  they  will  automatically  try  to  fill
themselves up immediately; think of it like a pump.

If you are grappling with a person on the ground or standing, you should try
to breathe in a relaxed manner except when you are attacking with a dynamic
and  explosive  technique.  Usually  you  would  push  and  grab  before  the
explosions  happen.  Remember  to  keep  breathing  while  doing  that;  if  you
don’t, you will get tired.

Relaxed breathing:

This type of breathing is usually used when you are out of breath. This could
be after doing a sprint or in a sparring situation. The technique is very simple
and  involves  you  breathing  in  and  filling  your  lungs  completely  over  the
course of 5 seconds. You then hold the breath for a second, and then slowly

exhale and completely empty your lungs again over the course of another 5
seconds.  Keep  this  pattern  going  as  long  as  necessary,  or  as  long  as  the
situation  allows.  If  you  are  extremely  winded  or  if  the  sparring  is  very
difficult,  try  doing  it  with  3-4  seconds  intervals.  Resist  the  temptation  to
break the rhythm by doing short and ineffective gasps. This is what untrained
people  do,  and  it  leads  to  greater  difficulty  catching  your  breath;  it  will
simply  take  longer.  Mastering  this  simple  technique  can  be  very  critical  if
you need to spar for extended periods of time.

Forceful breathing:

We  already  covered  the  short  burst  version  of  breathing;  another  kind  of
forceful breathing exits. This method can also be used as a way to catch your
breath,  and  it  is  also  used  as  a  way  to  train  yourself  for  the  short  burst
breathing you use when striking, i.e. the power of the strike. You start by first
making a quick and violent inhale. Some versions of this technique have the
arms go upwards at this point. Now your lungs are filled. What you do next is
to cringe and force the air slowly out of your lungs; force your tongue down
in your mouth, just below the teeth. If you are doing it right you should hear a
loud  sound  coming  from  your  throat,  a  hissing-type  noise.  Force  all  the  air
out  in  4  seconds  so  you  feel  empty.  Then  take  a  1  second  break,  and  then
force the rest of the air out violently. The arms usually go down at this point
in  the  technique.  Have  the  arms  continue  when  you  are  doing  your  little
break. The principle of this technique can also be done with a strike as a way
of  training  the  intensity  of  the  strike.  Inhale  and  then  slowly  perform  the
strike  for  the  4  seconds,  have  the  little  break,  and  then  force  out  the  last
air/power of the strike.
