## Natural Genome Editing & Biological “Upgrades”

Natural systems **edit genomes continuously**—not with intent, but via selectable mechanisms that bias survival and performance. Below is a compact, systems-level map.

---

### 1) Core Mechanisms (How Nature “Edits” DNA)

**A. Mutation**

- **Random base changes** during replication or from radiation/chemicals.
- Most are neutral; a small fraction improves fitness.

**B. Natural Selection**

- **Filter**, not a creator.
- Traits that increase survival/reproduction **accumulate** across generations.

**C. Genetic Recombination**

- Sexual reproduction reshuffles alleles → **new trait combinations** without new genes.

**D. Horizontal Gene Transfer (HGT)**

- Common in microbes.
- Entire genes move between organisms (e.g., antibiotic resistance).

**E. Epigenetic Regulation**

- DNA sequence unchanged.
- **Gene expression toggled** via methylation, histone modification.
- Can persist across generations (soft inheritance).

- **Trade-offs**: Strength vs. speed, growth vs. longevity, immunity vs. autoimmunity.
- **Timescale**: Generational, not individual.
