## DOMAIN I — FORCE DOMAIN (Raw Mechanical Output)

**Determines:** Total force production against external resistance

| Sub-Stat | Definition | Apex Method |
|---|---|---|
| **Absolute Strength** | Total force output regardless of bodyweight | Heavy compound lifts (1-5 reps), progressive load |
| **Relative Strength** | Force per kilogram bodyweight | Power-to-weight optimization, no excess mass |
| **Isometric Strength** | Static force production at fixed joint angle | Mid-range and end-range isometric holds |
| **Eccentric Strength** | Force absorption during lengthening | Slow controlled lowering (3-5s eccentric) |
| **Tendon Strength** | Force transmission from muscle to bone | Isometrics, heavy load, slow eccentric exposure |
| **Grip Strength** | Neurological and structural output indicator | Dead hangs, carries, heavy pulling |
| **Core Strength** | Force transfer stability across kinetic chain | Anti-rotation work, planks, loaded carries |

**Key law:** Relative strength > absolute strength for apex predatory use. Person B (70kg lifting 140kg) beats Person A (100kg lifting 150kg) in every real-world scenario.
