## 🧪 Exercise 1: Feynman Technique Practice

### **Objective:** Master the art of simple explanation

### **Instructions:**
1. Choose a complex concept from your R&D (e.g., "Long-Term Potentiation")
2. Set a timer for 5 minutes
3. Explain it as if teaching a 12-year-old
4. Record yourself or write it down
5. Identify where you used jargon or got stuck
6. Go back to source material for those gaps
7. Try again

### **Example:**

**Before (Jargon-heavy):**
> "LTP is a persistent strengthening of synapses based on recent patterns of activity, involving NMDA receptor activation and calcium influx."

**After (Feynman-style):**
> "Imagine your brain is like a forest with paths between trees. When you walk the same path over and over, it becomes wider and easier to travel. LTP is like that - when two brain cells talk to each other repeatedly, the connection between them gets stronger, making it easier for them to communicate next time."
