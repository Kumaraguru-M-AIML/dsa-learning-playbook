# Research Compendium: The Evidence Base

> **Purpose**: The foundational papers and scientific backing for the Master Protocol.
> **Status**: Reference Document

---

## 1. NEUROSCIENCE OF LEARNING

*   **Hebb, D. O. (1949).** *The Organization of Behavior*. (Foundational text on "Neurons that fire together, wire together" - Hebbian Learning).
*   **Kandel, E. R. (2001).** *The Molecular Biology of Memory Storage*. (Nobel Prize work on synaptic plasticity and LTP).
*   **Doidge, N. (2007).** *The Brain That Changes Itself*. (Seminal popular science on Neuroplasticity).
*   **Immordino-Yang, M. H., et al. (2012).** *Rest Is Not Idleness: Implications of the Brain’s Default Mode Network*. (Importance of DMN for consolidation/creativity).

## 2. COGNITIVE PSYCHOLOGY & MEMORY

*   **Roediger, H. L., & Butler, A. C. (2011).** *The critical role of retrieval practice in long-term retention*. (The "Testing Effect" gold standard).
*   **Cepeda, N. J., et al. (2006).** *Distributed practice in verbal recall tasks*. (Meta-analysis on Spaced Repetition efficiency).
*   **Bjork, R. A. (1994).** *Memory and metamemory considerations in the training of human beings*. (Concept of "Desirable Difficulties").
*   **Miller, G. A. (1956).** *The Magical Number Seven, Plus or Minus Two*. (Working Memory limits).

## 3. EXPERTISE & SKILL ACQUISITION

*   **Ericsson, K. A., et al. (1993).** *The role of deliberate practice in the acquisition of expert performance*. (The source of the "10,000 Hour Rule" concept, though nuanced).
*   **Bloom, B. S. (1984).** *The 2 Sigma Problem*. (Tutoring + Mastery Learning effectiveness).
*   **Wulf, G., et al. (2010).** *Frequent external-focus feedback enhances motor learning*. (Attentional focus in skill building).

## 4. METACOGNITION & MINDSET

*   **Dweck, C. S. (2006).** *Mindset: The New Psychology of Success*. (Growth vs. Fixed Mindset).
*   **Kruger, J., & Dunning, D. (1999).** *Unskilled and unaware of it*. (Metacognitive errors/Dunning-Kruger Effect).
*   **Duckworth, A. L., et al. (2007).** *Grit: Perseverance and passion for long-term goals*. (Resilience in learning).

## 5. SOCIAL & CONTEXTUAL LEARNING

*   **Vygotsky, L. S. (1978).** *Mind in Society*. (Zone of Proximal Development, Social Development Theory).
*   **Wenger, E. (1998).** *Communities of practice: Learning, meaning, and identity*. (Social learning clusters).
*   **Bandura, A. (1977).** *Social Learning Theory*. (Observational learning/Modeling).

## 6. ATTENTION & FOCUS

*   **Csikszentmihalyi, M. (1990).** *Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience*. (Flow state mechanics).
*   **Newport, C. (2016).** *Deep Work*. (Application of attention management in modern knowledge work).
*   **Kahneman, D. (1973).** *Attention and Effort*. (Cognitive resource theory).

## 7. DOMAIN-SPECIFIC LEARNING SCIENCE

*   **Krashen, S. (1982).** *Principles and Practice in Second Language Acquisition*. (The Input Hypothesis).
*   **Wolf, M. (2007).** *Proust and the Squid*. (The neuroscience of the reading brain).
*   **Newell, A., & Simon, H. A. (1972).** *Human Problem Solving*. (Problem space theory).
*   **Kintsch, W. (1998).** *Comprehension: A paradigm for cognition*. (Construction-Integration model).

---

## KEY TERMS INDEX

*   **Long-Term Potentiation (LTP)**: Strengthening of synapses based on recent patterns of activity.
*   **Myelination**: Insulation of axons to increase transmission speed (skill pathway reinforcement).
*   **Cognitive Load Theory**: Sweller's theory on working memory limits (Intrinsic, Extraneous, Germane load).
*   **Transfer Appropriate Processing**: Memory performance is best when processing at encoding matches processing at retrieval.
*   **Metacognition**: "Thinking about thinking" - monitoring and controlling one's own learning process.
