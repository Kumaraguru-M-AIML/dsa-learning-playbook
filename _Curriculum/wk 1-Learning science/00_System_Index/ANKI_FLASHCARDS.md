## 🧠 Neuroscience Fundamentals

### Card 1: Hebbian Learning
**Q:** What is the core principle of Hebbian learning?  
**A:** "Neurons that fire together, wire together" - repeated co-activation strengthens synaptic connections

**Tags:** #neuroscience #fundamentals #LTP

---

### Card 2: Long-Term Potentiation
**Q:** What is LTP and why does it matter for learning?  
**A:** Long-Term Potentiation - persistent strengthening of synapses based on repeated activity. It's the cellular mechanism of memory formation.

**Tags:** #neuroscience #memory #synapses

---

### Card 3: BDNF
**Q:** What is BDNF and how can you increase it?  
**A:** Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor - protein that supports neuron growth and synaptic plasticity. Increase via: aerobic exercise, learning new skills, fasting, sleep.

**Tags:** #neuroscience #exercise #neuroplasticity

---

### Card 4: Neuroplasticity Triggers
**Q:** What are the 3 main triggers for neuroplasticity?  
**A:** 1) Focused attention, 2) Deliberate practice, 3) Novelty and challenge

**Tags:** #neuroplasticity #learning #attention

---

### Card 5: Sleep Stages
**Q:** Which sleep stage consolidates declarative memories vs. procedural skills?  
**A:** Deep Sleep (NREM Stage 3) → Declarative memories (facts, events). REM Sleep → Procedural skills and emotional memories.

**Tags:** #sleep #memory #consolidation

---

## 🧩 Cognitive Psychology

### Card 6: Working Memory Capacity
**Q:** What is the current estimate for working memory capacity?  
**A:** ~4 chunks (Cowan's K), updated from Miller's 7±2

**Tags:** #cognitive-psychology #working-memory #capacity

---

### Card 7: Levels of Processing
**Q:** What are the three levels of processing and which is most effective?  
**A:** 1) Shallow (visual/acoustic), 2) Deep (semantic), 3) Elaborative (connections to prior knowledge). Elaborative is most effective.

**Tags:** #encoding #memory #processing

---

### Card 8: Testing Effect
**Q:** How much better is retrieval practice compared to re-reading?  
**A:** 50-60% better long-term retention

**Tags:** #retrieval-practice #testing-effect #memory

---

### Card 9: Spacing Effect
**Q:** What is the optimal spacing for the 3rd review session?  
**A:** 1 week after initial learning (Day 0 → Day 1 → Day 3 → Week 1 → Week 2 → Month 1)

**Tags:** #spaced-repetition #forgetting-curve #memory

---

### Card 10: Interleaving
**Q:** What is interleaving and why does it work?  
**A:** Mixing different problem types within a session (ABC-ABC-ABC vs AAA-BBB-CCC). Works by forcing discrimination between concepts and preventing autopilot.

**Tags:** #interleaving #practice #learning-strategies

---

### Card 11: Cognitive Load Types
**Q:** What are the 3 types of cognitive load?  
**A:** 1) Intrinsic (complexity of material), 2) Extraneous (poor instruction), 3) Germane (effort for learning). Minimize extraneous, optimize germane.

**Tags:** #cognitive-load #instructional-design #sweller

---

## 🎯 Attention & Focus

### Card 12: Flow State Conditions
**Q:** What are the 3 core conditions for entering flow state?  
**A:** 1) Clear goals, 2) Immediate feedback, 3) Challenge-skill balance (challenge = skill + 4%)

**Tags:** #flow #attention #csikszentmihalyi

---

### Card 13: Attention Residue
**Q:** How long does it take to regain full focus after an interruption?  
**A:** 20-23 minutes

**Tags:** #attention #deep-work #distraction

---

### Card 14: Ultradian Rhythms
**Q:** What is the optimal deep work block duration based on ultradian rhythms?  
**A:** 90-120 minutes

**Tags:** #attention #focus #productivity

---

### Card 15: Default Mode Network
**Q:** What is the DMN and when is it active?  
**A:** Default Mode Network - active during rest, mind-wandering, self-referential thought. Suppressed during focused work (Task-Positive Network active).

**Tags:** #neuroscience #attention #brain-networks

## 💪 Memory Techniques

### Card 16: Dual Coding Theory
**Q:** What is dual coding and why does it work?  
**A:** Combining verbal and visual information creates two separate memory traces, doubling retrieval probability.

**Tags:** #dual-coding #paivio #encoding

---

### Card 17: Elaborative Interrogation
**Q:** What question should you ask for elaborative interrogation?  
**A:** "Why is this true?" - forces causal elaboration and connection to prior knowledge

**Tags:** #elaboration #encoding #questioning

---

### Card 18: Chunking Mechanism
**Q:** How does chunking expand working memory capacity?  
**A:** Groups individual items into meaningful units, treating 5 items as 1 "chunk," freeing up WM slots for higher-order processing.

**Tags:** #chunking #working-memory #expertise

---

### Card 19: Feynman Technique Steps
**Q:** What are the 4 steps of the Feynman Technique?  
**A:** 1) Choose concept, 2) Teach it to a child (simple language), 3) Identify gaps, 4) Simplify and organize

**Tags:** #feynman #teaching #understanding

---

### Card 20: Emotional Encoding
**Q:** Which brain structure tags memories as emotionally salient?  
**A:** Amygdala - projects to hippocampus, lowering LTP threshold for "survival-relevant" information

**Tags:** #emotion #amygdala #memory

---

## 🎓 Learning Strategies

### Card 21: Deliberate Practice Components

**A:** 1) Specific goals, 2) Intense focus, 3) Immediate feedback, 4) Operating outside comfort zone

**Tags:** #deliberate-practice #ericsson #expertise

**A:** "I can't do this **yet**" - emphasizes ability to improve with effort

**Tags:** #growth-mindset #dweck #motivation

### Card 23: Self-Determination Theory

**A:** 1) Autonomy (control), 2) Competence (mastery), 3) Relatedness (connection)

**Tags:** #motivation #SDT #deci-ryan

### Card 24: Pomodoro Technique

**A:** 25 min work + 5 min break. Aligns with attention span, allows PFC recovery, creates dopamine reward loops.

**Tags:** #pomodoro #focus #productivity

### Card 25: Habit Loop

**A:** 1) Cue (trigger), 2) Routine (behavior), 3) Reward (positive outcome)

## 🔬 Advanced Concepts

### Card 26: Reward Prediction Error
**Q:** What is RPE and how does it affect learning?  
**A:** Reward Prediction Error - difference between expected and actual reward. Positive RPE → dopamine surge → learning. Negative RPE → dopamine dip → extinction.

**Tags:** #dopamine #learning #neuroscience

---

### Card 27: Expertise Reversal Effect
**Q:** What is the expertise reversal effect?  
**A:** Instruction optimal for novices (worked examples, scaffolding) can hinder experts. As expertise grows, reduce scaffolding.

**Tags:** #expertise #instructional-design #cognitive-load

---

### Card 28: Yerkes-Dodson Law
**Q:** What is the relationship between arousal and performance?  
**A:** Inverted U-curve: Low arousal → poor performance, Moderate arousal → optimal performance, High arousal → impaired performance

**Tags:** #stress #performance #arousal

---

### Card 29: Hippocampus Function
**Q:** What is the hippocampus's role in memory?  
**A:** Encodes new explicit memories, binds sensory inputs into coherent episodes, transfers memories to cortex during sleep

**Tags:** #hippocampus #memory #neuroscience

---

### Card 30: Prefrontal Cortex Functions
**Q:** What are the 4 main functions of the PFC?  
**A:** 1) Executive function (planning, decision-making), 2) Working memory, 3) Attention control, 4) Inhibition (impulse control)

**Tags:** #prefrontal-cortex #executive-function #neuroscience

---

## 📊 Meta-Learning

### Card 31: Focused vs Diffuse Mode
**Q:** When should you use focused vs diffuse mode?  
**A:** Focused mode for concentrated problem-solving. Diffuse mode (relaxed thinking) for making connections and insights. Alternate between them.

**Tags:** #meta-learning #oakley #thinking-modes

---

### Card 32: Metacognition Components
**Q:** What are the 4 metacognitive regulation skills?  
**A:** 1) Planning (setting goals), 2) Monitoring (checking progress), 3) Control (adjusting strategies), 4) Evaluating (assessing performance)

**Tags:** #metacognition #self-regulation #learning

---

### Card 33: Transfer of Learning
**Q:** What is "near transfer" vs "far transfer"?  
**A:** Near transfer: applying knowledge to similar contexts. Far transfer: applying to very different contexts (harder, requires deep understanding).

**Tags:** #transfer #learning #application

---

### Card 34: Forgetting Curve
**Q:** How much information is retained 24 hours after learning without review?  
**A:** ~30% (70% forgotten)

**Tags:** #forgetting-curve #ebbinghaus #memory

---

### Card 35: Progressive Overload
**Q:** What is the safe rate of progression per week?  
**A:** 2-5% increase per week (>10% risks injury/burnout)

**Tags:** #progressive-overload #skill-acquisition #practice
