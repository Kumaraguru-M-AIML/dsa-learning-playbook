# 🚀 THE 4-STEP ALGORITHM (The "Justin Sung" Engine)

Do not open a book and read Page 1. That is for amateurs. Use this algorithm for every single document in your library.

## STEP 1: PRIMING (The Scan) — *Time: 10 mins*
*   **Goal**: Build the scaffold before you pour the concrete.
*   **Action**: Use the **THIEVES** method on any PDF/Book.
    *   **T**itle
    *   **H**eadings
    *   **I**ntroduction (First paragraph)
    *   **E**very first sentence of every paragraph (Skim)
    *   **V**isuals (Graphs/Charts)
    *   **E**nd of chapter summaries
    *   **S**ummary (Conclusion)
*   **Result**: You now know the "shape" of the information.

## STEP 2: HYPOTHESIS & INQUIRY (The Hook) — *Time: 5 mins*
*   **Goal**: Create "neural hooks" for the data to latch onto.
*   **Action**: write down 3 specific questions you expect the text to answer.
    *   *Example*: "How does Dopamine relate to Effort?" "What is the difference between Schema and Metaphor?"
*   **Why**: Your brain ignores random data. It hunts for *answers*.

## STEP 3: ENCODING (The Connection) — *Time: 45-60 mins*
*   **Goal**: Connect new data to old data immediately.
*   **Action**: Do NOT take notes verbatim. Use **Concept Maps**.
    *   Draw the main idea in the center.
    *   Draw branches to related concepts.
    *   **CRITICAL**: Draw arrows connecting *this* book's ideas to *other* books you've read.
    *   *Example*: Connect "Atomic Habits" (James Clear) to "LTP" (Neuroscience).
*   **The Accelerator**: If you can't explain it simply, you didn't encode it. Pause and simplify.

## STEP 4: RETRIEVAL (The Cement) — *Time: 5 mins (End of Session)*
*   **Goal**: Stop the forgetting curve instantly.
*   **Action**: Close the book. Close your notes.
*   **The Blurting Method**: Take a blank sheet. Write down EVERYTHING you remember. Structure it.
*   **Feedback**: Check what you missed. *That* is your gap.
