# Domain Science: Curriculum Design

> **Status**: 🚀 System Infrastructure (Phase 6)
> **Focus**: The architecture of knowledge sequencing
> **Theoretical Basis**: Spiral Curriculum (Bruner), ZPD (Vygotsky), Cognitive Load Theory (Sweller)
> **Key Insight**: Order matters. The same information presented in the wrong sequence is noise; in the right sequence, it is learning.

---

## 1. THE MACRO STRUCTURE: THE SPIRAL CURRICULUM

### 🔬 Scientific Mechanism
**Jerome Bruner (1960)**: Do not wait until a student is "ready" to teach complex topics. Teach them *now* in a simplified form, and revisit them later.

1.  **Iterative Revisiting**: You must touch the same core concepts (e.g., "Gravity" or "Justice") multiple times over years.
2.  **Increasing Complexity**:
    *   *Loop 1 (Novice)*: Definitions & Examples (Intuitive).
    *   *Loop 2 (Intermediate)*: Mechanisms & Exceptions (Formal).
    *   *Loop 3 (Expert)*: Synthesis & Creative Application (Abstract).
3.  **Prior Knowledge Activation**: Each new loop must explicitly connect to the previous loop's anchor points.

---

## 2. THE MICRO STRUCTURE: SCAFFOLDING & ZPD

### 🔬 Scientific Mechanism
**Lev Vygotsky's Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD)**: Learning only happens in the "Goldilocks Zone" -> Too hard = Panic; Too easy = Boredom.
*   **Scaffolding**: Temporary support structures (e.g., hints, sentence starters, training wheels) that allow the learner to operate in the ZPD.
*   **Fading**: The critical step. You must systematically *remove* the scaffolds as competence grows. Failure to fade leads to "Prompt Dependence."

---

## 3. SEQUENCING LOGIC: SCOPE & SEQUENCE

### 🔧 Design Principles
*   **Scope**: *What* will be taught? (The breadth).
*   **Sequence**: *When* will it be taught? (The order).

#### Sequencing Models
1.  **Prerequisite-Based**: "Must know Addition before Multiplication." (Linear).
2.  **Whole-to-Part**: Show the big picture (The finished house), then teach the brick-laying. (Best for motivation/context).
3.  **Concrete-to-Abstract**: Start with physical manipulatives (Apples), move to Icons (Drawings), move to Symbols (Numbers). (Bruner's Enactive-Iconic-Symbolic).

## 4. COGNITIVE ARCHITECTURE IN DESIGN

### 🧠 Managing Cognitive Load (Sweller)
*   **Intrinsic Load**: The difficulty of the material itself. (Can't change this, must manage via Sequencing).
*   **Extraneous Load**: The "noise" of bad instruction (messy slides, confusing text). (ELIMINATE this).
*   **Germane Load**: The "good" effort of schematic construction. (MAXIMIZE this).

### ⚡ The "Worked Example Effect"
*   **Protocol**: For novices, solving problems is inefficient (too much cognitive load).
*   **Correction**: Show a fully worked solution first (Study), then a partially worked solution (Fade), then an unsolved problem (Solve).
*   **Why**: Borrows the expert's schema until the learner builds their own.

## 5. PROTOCOLS & TECHNIQUES

### 🏋️ Technique 1: The "Backward Design" (Wiggins & McTighe)
*   **Protocol**:
    1.  **Result**: What should they *be able to do* at the end? (Transfer Goal).
    2.  **Evidence**: How will I know they did it? (Assessment).
    3.  **Plan**: What creates that evidence? (Curriculum).
*   **Anti-Pattern**: Most people start with Step 3 ("I want to teach Chapter 1") without defining Step 1.

### 🏋️ Technique 2: The Syllabus Mapping
*   **Protocol**: Map your learning path.
    *   **Unit 1**: Foundations (Vocab + Big Picture).
    *   **Unit 2**: Core Mechanics (The "How").
    *   **Unit 3**: Edge Cases & Nuance (The "Except when...").
    *   **Unit 4**: Creative Transfer (Project).
