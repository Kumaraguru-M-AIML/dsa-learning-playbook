# Domain Science: Assessment & Feedback

> **Status**: 🚀 System Infrastructure (Phase 6)
> **Focus**: The science of measuring learning and accelerating it through loops
> **Theoretical Basis**: Assessment for Learning (Wiliam), Hattie & Timperley Model
> **Key Insight**: Assessment is not just a "dipstick" to measure oil; it is the act of *adding* oil.

---

## 1. THE CORE DISTINCTION: FORMATIVE VS. SUMMATIVE

### 🔬 Scientific Mechanism
*   **Summative Assessment (Assessment OF Learning)**:
    *   **Goal**: Verdict / Evaluation. (e.g., Final Exam).
    *   **Timing**: Autopsy (Post-mortem).
    *   **Effect on Learning**: Low to Negative (can induce "performance orientation" vs. "mastery orientation").
*   **Formative Assessment (Assessment FOR Learning)**:
    *   **Goal**: Feedback / Adjustment. (e.g., Mid-lesson check).
    *   **Timing**: Biopsy (Live diagnostic).
    *   **Effect on Learning**: High (provides the "error signal" needed for synaptic correction).
    *   **Dylan Wiliam's Rule**: An assessment is only formative if it *changes what you do next*.

---

## 2. THE FEEDBACK ENGINE: HATTIE & TIMPERLEY MODEL

### 🔬 Scientific Mechanism
Feedback is one of the most powerful influences on achievement (Effect Size = 0.70+), but only if it answers three specific questions:

1.  **Feed Up (Goal)**: "Where am I going?"
    *   *Failure Mode*: Student works hard without knowing what "Good" looks like.
2.  **Feed Back (Current)**: "How am I going?"
    *   *Failure Mode*: Vague praise ("Good job!") is toxic. It rewards the *ego*, not the *task*. Feedback must be specific ("Your pivot foot was too high").
3.  **Feed Forward (Next)**: "Where to next?"
    *   *Failure Mode*: Diagnosis without prescription.

### 🧠 The 4 Levels of Feedback
1.  **Task Level**: Correct/Incorrect. (Good for novices).
2.  **Process Level**: The method used. (Best for deep learning).
3.  **Self-Regulation Level**: "Did you check your work?" (Best for autonomy).
4.  **Self Level**: "You are smart!" (❌ **AVOID**: Reduces resilience and creates fixed mindset).

## 3. PROTOCOLS & TECHNIQUES

### 🏋️ Technique 1: The "Hinge Point Question" (Wiliam)
*   **Protocol**: A multiple-choice diagnostic designed so that *every wrong answer* predicts a specific misconception.
*   **Usage**: Ask it mid-session.
    *   If Answer A: Do nothing (Correct).
    *   If Answer B: Re-explain Concept X.
    *   If Answer C: Re-explain Concept Y.
*   **Why**: Immediate loop closure.

### 🏋️ Technique 2: "Two Stars and a Wish" (Peer Feedback)
*   **Protocol**: Peer review.
    *   **2 Stars**: Identify two things that met the criteria perfectly.
    *   **1 Wish**: Identify one specific thing to change for next time.
*   **Why**: Trains the reviewer's critical eye (Assessment Literacy) and lowers the social threat for the receiver.

### 🏋️ Technique 3: Rubric Parsing
*   **Protocol**: Show the learner the rubric *before* the task. Have them grade a "model performance" using the rubric.
*   **Mechanism**: "Feed Up". Aligns the internal error-detection, so the student can self-correct during the work.

---

## 4. DESIGNING VALID ASSESSMENTS

### 🔑 Key Variables
*   **Validity**: Does it measure what it claims to measure? (e.g., Does a multiple-choice history test measure "critical thinking"? Probably not).
*   **Reliability**: Would I get the same score if I took it tomorrow?
*   **Transfer**: Can I apply this skill in a non-test environment?
