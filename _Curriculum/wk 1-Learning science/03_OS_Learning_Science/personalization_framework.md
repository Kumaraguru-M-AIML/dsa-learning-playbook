# Personalization Framework: Adapting the Science

> **Purpose**: Customizing the Master Protocol for individual differences. One size does not fit all.
> **Status**: Synthesis Document

---

## 1. EXPERTISE LEVEL: Novice vs. Expert

**The "Expertise Reversal Effect"**: Techniques that help novices can hurt experts, and vice versa.

### A. The Novice Support Phase
*   **Need**: Structure, guidance, reduced cognitive load.
*   **Do**:
    *   **Worked Examples**: Study full solutions before solving.
    *   **Scaffolding**: Use templates, checklists, and guides.
    *   **Immediate Feedback**: Correct errors instantly to prevent fossilization.
*   **Avoid**: Pure discovery learning (too much cognitive load).

### B. The Expert Autonomy Phase
*   **Need**: Autonomy, challenge, discovery.
*   **Do**:
    *   **Discovery Learning**: Solve problems with minimal guidance.
    *   **Interleaving**: High interference practice to refine discrimination.
    *   **Delayed Feedback**: Encourages self-error-detection.
*   **Avoid**: Over-structured guidance (becomes redundant and distracting).

## 2. NEURODIVERSITY ADJUSTMENTS

### A. ADHD / Executive Function Challenges
*   **Challenge**: Sustaining attention, working memory, starting tasks.
*   **Protocol**:
    *   **Externalize Executive Function**: Don't hold plans in head. Write them down visibly.
    *   **Micro-Pomodoros**: Shorten work blocks (10-15 mins) if 25 is too hard.
    *   **Kinesthetic Learning**: Fidget tools, standing desk, walk while reading.
    *   **Body Doubling**: Work alongside someone (accountability partner) to anchor focus.

### B. Anxiety / High Stress
*   **Challenge**: Cortisol blocks retrieval and prefrontal cortex function.
*   **Protocol**:
    *   **Safety Framing**: Reframe errors as "data points" not "failures".
    *   **Low-Stakes Testing**: Practice retrieval in private before public performance.
    *   **Physiological Regulation**: Use "Physiological Sigh" before study to downregulate nervous system.

### C. Dyslexia / Processing Differences
*   **Challenge**: Phonological processing, text-heavy input.
*   **Protocol**:
    *   **Multi-Sensory Input**: Text-to-speech + Reading simultaneously.
    *   **Visual Reliance**: Use mind maps, diagrams, and videos over dense text.
    *   **Oral Output**: Use voice dictation for drafting ideas.

## 3. CHRONOTYPE & ENERGY (Biological Timing)

### A. The "Lark" (Morning Type)
*   **Peak**: 7:00 AM - 11:00 AM.
*   **Protocol**: Do "Deep Work" (Coding, Writing, Heavy Reading) immediately upon waking. Leave admin/meetings for afternoon slump.

### B. The "Owl" (Evening Type)
*   **Peak**: 8:00 PM - 12:00 AM.
*   **Protocol**: Don't force 8 AM study. Use morning for sunlight (to regulate clock) and routine movement. specific Deep Work block late at night.

### C. The "Third Bird" (Intermediate)
*   **Peak**: 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM & 4:00 PM - 6:00 PM.
*   **Protocol**: Standard 9-5 rhythm often works, but beware the "Post-Lunch Dip" (1-3 PM). Schedule rest or low-load tasks then.

## 4. PERSONALITY TRAITS (Big Five)

### A. High Openness
*   **Strength**: Curiosity, connecting ideas. **Weakness**: Distractibility, "Shiny Object Syndrome".
*   **Fix**: **Constraint-Based Creativity**. Give yourself strict time/topic limits to channel curiosity.

### B. High Conscientiousness
*   **Strength**: Discipline, planning. **Weakness**: Rigidity, perfectionism.
*   **Fix**: **Time-Boxed Perfection**. Set a timer for "polishing". When it rings, ship it.

### C. High Neuroticism
*   **Strength**: Vigilance, identifying risks. **Weakness**: Anxiety, fear of failure.
*   **Fix**: **Defensive Pessimism**. "What is the worst that can happen?" Plan for it, then proceed.
