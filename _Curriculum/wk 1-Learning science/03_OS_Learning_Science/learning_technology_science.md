# Domain Science: Learning Technology

> **Status**: 🚀 System Infrastructure (Phase 6)
> **Focus**: The interface between human cognition and digital systems
> **Theoretical Basis**: Multimedia Learning Theory (Mayer), Gamification (SDT), AI Tutoring
> **Key Insight**: Technology should not "deliver" content; it should "extend" cognition.

---

## 1. THE USER INTERFACE: MULTIMEDIA LEARNING THEORY

### 🔬 Scientific Mechanism
**Richard Mayer's Principles**: The brain processes information in two channels (Visual/Auditory) with limited capacity. Good tech design respects these limits.

1.  **Coherence Principle**: "Less is More." Removing interesting but irrelevant details (background music, decorative images) *increases* learning.
2.  **Contiguity Principle**: Text and corresponding images must be near each other. If the eye has to scan back and forth, you increase **Extraneous Load**.
3.  **Signaling Principle**: Highlight essential material (bold text, arrows) to guide the Attentional Spotlight.
4.  **Modality Principle**: People learn better from Graphics + Narration than from Graphics + Text. (Narration uses the Auditory loop, leaving the Visual loop free for the image).

## 2. THE ENGINE: GAMIFICATION & MOTIVATION

### 🔬 Scientific Mechanism
Gamification is often misunderstood as "Badges and Points." Real gamification targets **Self-Determination Theory (SDT)** needs:
1.  **Autonomy**: The user must feel they have a choice (e.g., skill trees, multiple paths).
2.  **Competence**: The challenge must scale with skill (Flow Channel).
3.  **Relatedness**: Social comparison or cooperation.

*   **The Octalysis Framework (Yu-kai Chou)**: Distinguishes between "White Hat" (Empowerment, Meaning) and "Black Hat" (Scarcity, Fear of Loss) drives. Sustainable learning tech uses White Hat.

## 3. THE TUTOR: ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE (AI)

### 🔬 Scientific Mechanism
**The "2 Sigma" Problem (Bloom)**: One-on-one tutoring improves performance by 2 standard deviations (moving an average student to the top 2%). AI aims to scale this.

*   **Intelligent Tutoring Systems (ITS)**:
    *   **Student Model**: The AI tracks the student's current knowledge state (Bayesian Knowledge Tracing).
    *   **Pedagogical Model**: The AI decides *how* to teach (e.g., "They failed step 3, give a hint" vs. "Give the answer").
    *   **Domain Model**: The expert knowledge base.
*   **Generative AI Role**: Acts as an "Infinite Socratic Partner," allowing the student to engage in **Active Recall** and **Elaboration** via dialogue, rather than passive consumption.

## 4. PROTOCOLS & TECHNIQUES

### 🏋️ Technique 1: The "Cognitive Offloading" Audit
*   **Protocol**: Check your tools. Are they *doing the thinking* for you, or *enabling* your thinking?
    *   *Bad Tech*: GPS (Weakens spatial memory).
    *   *Good Tech*: Anki (Strengthens memory retrieval).
*   **Rule**: Use tech for **Storage** and **Scheduling**, not for **Processing**.

### 🏋️ Technique 2: The "Just-in-Time" Learning Stack
*   **Protocol**: Use search/AI only when you hit an **Impasse** (Problem Solving).
*   **Mechanism**: The impasse creates a "knowledge gap" (curiosity) that makes the subsequent answer stickier (Dopamine prediction error).
