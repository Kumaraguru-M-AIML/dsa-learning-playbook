# Domain Science: Problem Solving

> **Status**: 🚀 Frontier Document (New R&D)
> **Focus**: The cognitive architecture of overcoming obstacles
> **Theoretical Basis**: Problem Space Theory (Newell & Simon), Representational Change Theory (Ohlsson), Analogical Transfer
> **Neuroscience Focus**: Anterior Cingulate Cortex (Insight), Prefrontal Cortex (Search)

---

## 1. THE FOUNDATION: PROBLEM SPACE THEORY

### 🔬 Scientific Mechanism
**Newell & Simon (1972)**: Problem solving is a search through a "space."
1.  **Initial State**: Where you are.
2.  **Goal State**: Where you want to be.
3.  **Operators**: Valid moves you can make.
4.  **Path Constraints**: Rules you cannot break.

*   **Means-Ends Analysis**: The primary heuristic. The brain calculates the "distance" to the goal and selects an operator to reduce that distance.
    *   *Trap*: Sometimes you must increase the distance (move backward) to solve the problem (e.g., Tower of Hanoi, Rubik's Cube). Hill-climbing algorithms fail here.

---

## 2. THE ENGINE: INSIGHT (Representational Change)

### 🔬 Scientific Mechanism
**Ohlsson's Theory**: Why do we get stuck? Because our initial **Representation** of the problem is wrong. We are searching the *wrong* problem space.
*   **Impasse**: The state of being stuck.
*   **Insight (Aha!)**: Sudden restructuring of the problem representation.
    1.  **Constraint Relaxation**: Realizing a rule you thought existed doesn't actually exist (e.g., Nine Dots Puzzle).
    2.  **Chunk Decomposition**: Breaking a perceptual chunk into components (e.g., seeing a "tack box" as a "box" and "tacks" separately).

### 🧠 Neuroscience of Insight
*   **Right Anterior Superior Temporal Gyrus (aSTG)**: Fires during the "Aha!" moment (semantic integration).
*   **Anterior Cingulate Cortex (ACC)**: Detects the "conflict" or "impasse" prior to the switch.
*   **Alpha Gating**: The brain momentarily "blinks" (shuts off visual input) just before an insight to look inward.

---

## 3. ANALOGICAL TRANSFER

### 🔬 Scientific Mechanism
**Mapping Structure**: Solving a novel target problem by retrieving a familiar source problem with the same *deep structure* but different *surface features*.
*   **The Dunker Radiation Problem**: How to kill a tumor without killing tissue?
    *   *Analogy*: General attacking a fortress (divide the army).
    *   *Solution*: Divide the ray beam.
*   **The Failure**: Novices focus on surface features (Laser vs. Army). Experts focus on structural features (Divide & Converge).

### 🔑 Key Variables
*   **Encoding Specificity**: To transfer later, you must encode the *principle*, not just the *solution*, during the initial learning.

---

## 4. OBSTACLES: FUNCTIONAL FIXEDNESS

### 🔬 Scientific Mechanism
**The Candle Problem (Duncker)**: Giving someone a box of tacks makes them see the box *as a container*. Emptying the box first makes them see it *as a shelf*.
*   **Cognitive Economy**: The brain tags objects with their "canonical use" to save energy. Overcoming this requires suppressing the "primary definition."

---

## 5. PROTOCOLS & TECHNIQUES

### 🏋️ Technique 1: The "10% Move" (Breaking Hill Climbing)
*   **Protocol**: If stuck, deliberately make a move that increases the distance to the goal (makes the problem worse).
*   **Mechanism**: Forces the brain out of a "Local Maximum" trap.

### 🏋️ Technique 2: "In Other Words" (Representational Shifting)
*   **Protocol**: Re-state the problem in 3 radically different ways.
    *   *Visual*: Draw it.
    *   *Physical*: Build it.
    *   *Metaphorical*: "This is like..."
*   **Mechanism**: Actively triggers Representational Change Theory by forcing new encoding.

### 🏋️ Technique 3: Incubation (The Shower Effect)
*   **Protocol**: Work until Impasse. STOP. Do something low-load (walk, shower).
*   **Mechanism**: Allows "Spreading Activation" to reach weaker, distant associations that were inhibited by the loud "Focused Mode" execution.
