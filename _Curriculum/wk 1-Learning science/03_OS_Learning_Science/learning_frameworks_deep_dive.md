# Learning Frameworks: Deep Dive

> **Status**: R&D Reference Document
> **Focus**: Major Learning Theories (How Learning Happens)
> **Goal**: To understand the underlying mechanisms of knowledge acquisition.

---

## 1. ACTIVITY THEORY (Engeström/Vygotsky)

### 🔬 The Concept
Learning is not an isolated mental act. It is a social, object-oriented activity mediated by tools.
*   **The Triangle**: Subject (Learner) <-> Object (Goal) <-> Community.
*   **Mediation**: You cannot reach the Object directly; you use "Tools" (Language, Software, Models).

### 📐 The Expansion (Engeström)
1.  **Tools**: Instruments used (Tech, Language).
2.  **Rules**: Norms/Constraints (Deadlines, Syntax).
3.  **Community**: Who else is involved? (Team, Peers).
4.  **Division of Labor**: Who does what?

### 🎓 Application: "Expansive Learning"
*   **Problem**: When the system breaks (Contradiction), learning happens.
*   **Protocol**: don't just "teach code." Teach the *System*. Who uses the code? What are the rules? What are the tools? true mastery is maximizing the *entire* triangle.

## 2. SITUATED COGNITION (Lave & Wenger)

### 🔬 The Concept
"Knowing" is inseparable from "Doing." Learning is **Legitimate Peripheral Participation (LPP)**.
*   **Context**: You cannot learn vocabulary effectively from a list; you learn it by *being in the country*.
*   **Enculturation**: Learning is becoming a member of a "Community of Practice."

### 🎓 Application: "The Apprenticeship"
*   **Novice**: Starts at the edge (Peripheral). Simple, low-risk tasks (Observation, basic maintenance).
*   **Expert**: Moves to the center. High-risk, complex tasks.
*   **Rule**: Stop teaching "Abstract Concepts" first. Teach "Situated Actions." (e.g., Don't teach "What is a Variable?" Teach "How do we store this user's name?").

## 3. COGNITIVE APPRENTICESHIP (Collins et al.)

### 🔬 The Concept
Applying the traditional apprenticeship model (Tailor/Blacksmith) to *cognitive* skills (Math, Reading, Coding).

### The Phases
1.  **Modeling**: Expert performs the task *loudly* (Thinking Aloud). "I am doing X because Y..."
2.  **Coaching**: Learner performs. Expert gives feedback (Guidance).
3.  **Scaffolding**: Expert provides supports (Hints, Templates).
4.  **Fading**: Expert slowly removes supports.
5.  **Articulation**: Learner explains their reasoning.
6.  **Reflection**: Learner compares their process to the expert's.
7.  **Exploration**: Learner defines their own problems.

## 4. SELF-REGULATED LEARNING (SRL) - (Zimmerman)

### 🔬 The Concept
The difference between a student and a master. Masters control their own learning process.

### The Cyclic Model
1.  **Forethought Phase**:
    *   *Task Analysis*: Goal setting, strategic planning.
    *   *Self-Motivation*: "Can I do this?" (Efficacy).
2.  **Performance Phase**:
    *   *Self-Control*: Mental imagery, attention focusing.
    *   *Self-Observation*: Metacognitive monitoring ("Am I understanding this?").
3.  **Self-Reflection Phase**:
    *   *Self-Judgment*: Evaluation against standard.
    *   *Self-Reaction*: Adaptation. "I need to change my strategy."

### 🎓 Application
*   **The "Metacognitive Wrap"**: Never just "do a problem."
    *   *Pre*: What is my plan?
    *   *During*: Is the plan working?
    *   *Post*: Why did it work/fail?

## 5. CONSTRUCTIVISM (Piaget/Vygotsky)

### 🔬 The Concept
Humans *construct* meaning; they do not *record* it.
*   **Assimilation**: Fitting new info into old boxes.
*   **Accommodation**: Breaking the box to fit new info.
*   **ZPD**: The zone where learning happens (Too hard to do alone, easy to do with help).

---

## 6. CONNECTIVISM (Siemens)
*   **Network Theory**: Learning is the ability to traverse/create networks.
*   **Currency**: The capacity to *know more* is more important than what is currently known.

---

## 7. TRANSFER OF LEARNING
*   **Near Transfer**: Driving Ford -> Driving Toyota.
*   **Far Transfer**: Chess -> Business Strategy. (Requires deliberate abstraction).
