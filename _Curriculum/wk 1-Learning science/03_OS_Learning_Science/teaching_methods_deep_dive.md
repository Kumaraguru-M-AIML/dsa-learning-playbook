## 1. GAMIFICATION (Mechanics & Dynamics)

### 🔬 What Is It?
The application of game-design elements and game principles in non-game contexts. It is **not** just adding points and badges; it is about engineering *motivation*.

### The MDA Framework (Hunicke et al.)
1.  **Mechanics** (The Rules): Points, Levels, Leaderboards, Badges.
2.  **Dynamics** (The Behavior): Competition, Cooperation, Collection, Surprise.
3.  **Aesthetics** (The Feeling): Discovery, Challenge, Fellowship, Submission (Flow).

### 🎓 Teaching Application
*   **Progress Indicators**: Humans need to "see" progress. Use XP bars instead of grades. Grants a sense of *competence*.
*   **"Quest" Structure**: Frame assignments as missions. "Defeat the Boss" (Final Exam).
*   **Instant Feedback Loop**: Games give feedback every second. Classrooms give it every week. Close the gap.

## 2. PROBLEM-BASED LEARNING (PBL)

### 🔬 What Is It?
Student-centered pedagogy in which students learn about a subject through the experience of solving an open-ended problem.

### The Maastricht Seven Jump Steps
1.  **Clarify terms**: What do we know? What acts are unclear?
2.  **Define the problem**: Formulate the core question.
3.  **Brainstorm**: Generate hypothesis.
4.  **Structure**: Organize ideas systematically.
5.  **Formulate learning objectives**: What do we *need* to research to solve this?
6.  **Self-Study**: Go away and research (The Learning Phase).
7.  **Synthesis**: Come back and apply findings to solving the problem.

### 🎓 Teaching Application
*   **Start with the Why**: Don't teach the formula first. Present the *disaster* that happens if you don't have the formula.

## 3. INQUIRY-BASED LEARNING (The 5E Model)

### 🔬 What Is It?
A constructivist approach where learners build knowledge through questioning and research.

### The 5E Cycle (Bybee)
1.  **Engage**: Capture interest (The Hook).
2.  **Explore**: Hands-on activities/research without direct instruction.
3.  **Explain**: Students articulate their understanding; Teacher clarifies *after* exploration.
4.  **Elaborate**: Apply concepts to new contexts (Transfer).
5.  **Evaluate**: Assess understanding.

## 4. DIRECT INSTRUCTION (Rosenshine's Principles)

### 🔬 What Is It?
Explicit teaching. Systematic, structured, and effective for novices. "I do, We do, You do."

### Rosenshine’s 10 Principles
1.  **Daily Review**: Re-activate neural pathways.
2.  **Small Steps**: Reduce cognitive load.
3.  **Ask Questions**: Check for understanding constantly.
4.  **Models**: Provide worked examples (Schema building).
5.  **Guide Practice**: "We do."
6.  **Check for Success**: 80% success rate before moving on.
7.  **Scaffold**: Support difficult tasks.
8.  **Independent Practice**: "You do." (Overlearning/Automaticity).
9.  **Weekly/Monthly Review**: Spaced repetition.

## 5. SOCRATIC METHOD
*   **Concept**: Teaching by asking questions.
*   **Goal**: To expose contradictions in the student's mental model ("The Midwife of Ideas").

---

## 6. FEYNMAN TECHNIQUE
*   **Concept**: Interpretive translation. "If you can't explain it to a 6-year-old, you don't understand it."
*   **Goal**: To identify gaps in understanding and remove jargon masking.

---

## 7. DUAL CODING (Paivio)
*   **Concept**: Brain has two channels (Visual + Verbal).
*   **Rule**: Presenting both simultaneously doubles the strength of the memory trace.

---

## 8. DELIBERATE PRACTICE (Ericsson)
*   **Concept**: Intense, focused repetition of *sub-skills* with immediate feedback.
*   **Goal**: To build "Mental Representations" (Schemas) that allow for automaticity.
