# Domain Science: Developmental Learning (Age-Based)

> **Status**: 🚀 System Infrastructure (Phase 6)
> **Focus**: How learning hardware changes from birth to old age
> **Theoretical Basis**: Critical Periods, Neuroplasticity Types, Fluid vs. Crystallized Intelligence
> **Key Insight**: Children are "Lanterns" (diffuse attention); Adults are "Spotlights" (focused attention).

---

## 1. CHILDHOOD (0-12): THE LANTERN (Exploration)

### 🔬 Scientific Mechanism
*   **Synaptic Overproduction**: Infants have *trillions* more synapses than adults.
*   **Critical Periods**: Windows of time where the brain expects specific input (e.g., phonemes, vision).
    *   *Language*: Closes around age 7-12 (for native-like accent).
    *   *Vision*: Closes very early.
*   **Mechanism**: **Passive Plasticity**. The brain absorbs everything without effort (LTP threshold is low).
*   **Gopnik's "Lantern"**: Attention is diffuse. They are bad at focusing on *one* thing because they are designed to notice *everything*.

---

## 2. ADOLESCENCE (13-24): THE PRUNING (Specialization)

### 🔬 Scientific Mechanism
*   **Synaptic Pruning**: "Use it or lose it." The brain aggressively cuts weak connections (up to 50% in some areas) to increase efficiency.
*   **Myelination**: White matter (insulation) grows, increasing speed 100x.
*   **Reward System Sensitivity**: The Nucleus Accumbens (Reward) matures *before* the Prefrontal Cortex (Brakes). High risk-taking, high social drive.

---

## 3. ADULTHOOD (25-60): THE SPOTLIGHT (Execution)

### 🔬 Scientific Mechanism
*   **Active Plasticity**: The brain is no longer "absorbent." Structural change now requires **Focus** (Acetylcholine) and **Effort** (Norepinephrine).
*   **Fluid vs. Crystallized Intelligence** (Cattell):
    *   **Fluid (Gf)**: Processing speed / Novel problem solving. Peaks at 25, declines slowly.
    *   **Crystallized (Gc)**: Vocabulary / Accumulated wisdom. Keep rising until 60+.
*   **Andragogy (Knowles)**: Adults learn differently.
    1.  **Need to Know**: Adults must know *why* they are learning.
    2.  **Experience**: They bring mental models (schemas) that must be respected or debugged.

---

## 4. ELDERHOOD (60+): THE PRESERVATION (Maintenance)

### 🔬 Scientific Mechanism
*   **Neurogenesis**: Yes, the hippocampus still grows new neurons (Dentate Gyrus), but at a slower rate.
*   **Cognitive Reserve**: The "Hardware" creates workarounds. High-performing elders recruit *bilateral* brain regions to do tasks that youth do with one side (HAROLD Model).
*   **Super-Agers**: Individuals in their 80s with brains of 50-year-olds. Key factor: **Novelty + Effort**. (Doing crossword puzzles isn't enough; you must learn a new language or instrument).

---

## 5. PROTOCOLS & TECHNIQUES

### 🏋️ Technique 1: The "Adult Focus" Protocol
*   **Context**: You are not a child sponge. You need chemicals to learn.
*   **Protocol**:
    1.  **Agitation**: Embrace the frustration (Norepinephrine).
    2.  **Focus**: Spotlight attention (Acetylcholine).
    3.  **Rest**: Deep sleep (Consolidation).

### 🏋️ Technique 2: The "Protective Reserve" Strategy
*   **Context**: Managing cognitive decline.
*   **Protocol**: High-Aerobic Exercise (BDNF release) + High-Novelty Learning.
*   **Mechanism**: BDNF acts as "Miracle-Gro" for the hippocampus, combating atrophy.
