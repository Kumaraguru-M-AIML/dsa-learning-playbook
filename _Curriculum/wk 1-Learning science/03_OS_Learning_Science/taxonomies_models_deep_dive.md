# Taxonomies & Models: Deep Dive

> **Status**: R&D Reference Document
> **Focus**: Hierarchical Structures & Assessment Models
> **Goal**: To measure the "Depth" of learning, not just the "Presence" of it.

---

## 1. THE SOLO TAXONOMY (Structure of Observed Learning Outcomes)

### 🔬 The Model (Biggs & Collis)
Unlike Bloom's (which categorizes *objectives*), SOLO categorizes *performance*. It describes the **structural complexity** of a student's answer.

### The 5 Levels
1.  **Pre-structural**: "I don't know." Misses the point entirely.
2.  **Uni-structural**: Focuses on **one** relevant aspect. (e.g., "Photosynthesis makes air.")
3.  **Multi-structural**: Focuses on **several** relevant aspects, but they are treated independently ("List" thinking). No integration.
4.  **Relational**: Aspects are integrated into a structure. Explains causes, effects, and parts-to-whole relationships.
5.  **Extended Abstract**: Generalizes the structure to a new domain. Hypothesizes. (Transfer).

### 🎓 Teaching Application
*   **Assessment**: Do not ask "Did they get it right?" Ask "What is the structural complexity of their response?"
*   **The "One Plus" Rule**: To teach, identify the student's current level (e.g., Multi-structural) and scaffold the *next* level (Relational - "How do these list items connect?").

## 2. MILLER'S PYRAMID (Clinical/Skill Competence)

### 🔬 The Model
Designed for medical education, but applies to all **Performance Skills** (Coding, Design, Engineering).

### The 4 Levels
1.  **Knows** (Knowledge): Fact gathering. "I know the code syntax." -> *Assessment: Written Test.*
2.  **Knows How** (Competence): Analysis/Interpretation. "I know how to write a function to solve X." -> *Assessment: Case Study.*
3.  **Shows How** (Performance): Demonstration in vitro. "I can write the code in a sandbox environment." -> *Assessment: Simulation/OSCE.*
4.  **Does** (Action): Performance in vivo. "I write the code in production." -> *Assessment: Workplace Observation.*

### 🎓 Teaching Application
*   **Gap Analysis**: Most education stops at Level 2 ("Knows How"). True mastery requires Level 3 & 4.
*   **Method Check**: You cannot measure "Does" with a Multiple Choice Question.

## 3. BLOOM'S TAXONOMY (Revised)

### 🔬 The Model (Anderson & Krathwohl, 2001)
The classic functionality hierarchy.

1.  **Remember**: Retrieve knowledge.
2.  **Understand**: Construct meaning.
3.  **Apply**: Use procedure in given situation.
4.  **Analyze**: Break into parts, determine relationships.
5.  **Evaluate**: Make judgments based on criteria.
6.  **Create**: Put elements together to form a novel whole.

### The Hidden Dimension: Knowledge Types
Bloom's is not just 6 verbs; it's a grid intersecting with 4 knowledge types:
1.  **Factual**: Terminology.
2.  **Conceptual**: Classifications, principles, theories.
3.  **Procedural**: Skills, algorithms, techniques.
4.  **Metacognitive**: Knowledge of self and cognitive strategies.

*   *Mastery Goal*: Moving the learner from **Factual/Remember** -> **Metacognitive/Create**.

## 4. MARZANO’S NEW TAXONOMY

### 🔬 The Model
Critique of Bloom: Bloom ignored the "Self." A student can have the *skill* to Evaluate (Level 5) but if they lack the *motivation* to start, the cognition never happens.

### The 3 Systems
1.  **The Self-System**: "Do I want to do this? Can I do this?" (Motivation/Efficacy). *If this is No, the process stops.*
2.  **The Metacognitive System**: "How will I do this?" (Goal setting, monitoring).
3.  **The Cognitive System**: "Processing the info." (Retrieval, Comprehension, Analysis, Use).

### 🎓 Teaching Application
*   **Debug Protocol**: If a learner is failing, check the Self-System *first*. It is the gatekeeper.

## 5. KIRKPATRICK MODEL (Evaluation of Training)

### 🔬 The Model
How do we know the specific R&D or Course worked?

1.  **Reaction**: Did they like it? (Surveys). *Weak correlation with learning.*
2.  **Learning**: Did they acquire knowledge/skill? (Tests).
3.  **Behavior**: Did they apply it on the job? (Transfer).
4.  **Results**: Did it impact the business/life goal? (ROI).
